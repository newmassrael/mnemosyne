Knowledge breaks (scene-id : who knows/ignores what, and why it's wrong): none
One paragraph: Taken whole this is a coherent authored story with a living cast and four genuinely distinct roads, not a pile of facts. A single buried ground truth (Maddox, secretly dying, skimmed for years, framed the innocent collier on the ledger's last page in a disguised hand, then dosed himself with the herbwife's tincture the night the factor confronted him) is refracted through a dozen partial minds, each holding only what their own road earns, and the three forks (ration-vs-ledger, village-vs-factor, law-vs-settle) drive the winter to four endings that truly diverge and stay diverged. The cost of that careful design is a heavy 20-scene shared trunk and a handful of setups (the soldier's vigil, the child's sighting, the prospectors, the sick servant) that pay off on some roads and simply lapse on others.
