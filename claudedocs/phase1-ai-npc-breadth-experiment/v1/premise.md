# Premise — "The Lantern House at Corvath Ford"

This is the ONLY story input you are given. Everything else — the events, each
person's secrets, what is really happening, the scene sequence, the branches, the
causality, and (above all) WHO KNOWS WHAT — is yours to invent.

## Setting

Corvath Ford is the only crossing of the Tessit river for forty miles — a stone
waystation (the Lantern House) with a stable yard, a common room, and a handful of
sleeping cells, kept by a ford-warden who raises and lowers the chain-ferry and
lights the river lantern at dusk. In early spring the snowmelt comes down hard and
the ford floods; when it does, whoever is at the Lantern House is stranded together
until the water falls — usually three or four days. This spring the flood has caught
a full house: a dozen travellers who do not know each other, each crossing for their
own reasons, now shut in together while the river runs too high to cross.

## The cast (you may rename, reshape, or add — but keep it BROAD)

A waystation full of strangers is the point. These are seeds; make each one a real
person with their own knowledge, their own reason for being on the road, and their
own stake in what happens. At minimum these, and feel free to add more:

- **Wend Aldercott** — the deputy ford-warden, young, left in charge while the old
  warden is away downriver. The point-of-view character. Earnest, out of their depth.
- **the old ford-warden** — absent at the start; what they were doing matters.
- **a circuit magistrate's clerk** — travelling to the assize at the county town,
  carrying sealed papers.
- **a horse-trader** and **a drover** — travelling together, or pretending to.
- **a midwife** returning from a birth two valleys over.
- **a discharged soldier** walking home from a war that may or may not be over.
- **a wool-factor** (a merchant's agent) with a strongbox and a ledger.
- **a young couple** crossing to be married against a family's wishes.
- **a tinker** who has crossed at Corvath a hundred times and knows the warden.
- **a hooded traveller** who keeps to their cell and gives no name.

## The inciting event

On the first night of the flood, something happens at the Lantern House that should
not have — a death, a theft, a disappearance, a fire, a body in the river, a forged
seal, a sealed dispatch gone missing: YOU choose the inciting event and make it true
in the facts. It is witnessed by some and not others; it is understood differently by
nearly everyone. Several of the stranded travellers know a piece of what happened;
none of them knows all of it; some of them are lying about what they know.

## The central dramatic question

Wend, left in charge, must work out what happened — from a houseful of people who
each hold a different fragment and a different agenda — and decide what to do before
the water falls and everyone scatters across the ford. When the river drops, Wend can:

- **NAME** — lay the matter before the circuit clerk (or the returning warden, or the
  magistrate at the county town) as Wend has come to understand it, and let the law
  take it;
- **HOLD** — keep what Wend has learned quiet (to protect someone, to avoid a wrong
  accusation, or out of doubt), and let the travellers cross unhindered;
- **ACT** — settle the matter at the Lantern House itself before anyone crosses,
  Wend's own way.

What is really behind the inciting event, what each traveller actually knows and
believes, and where each of these three roads leads — all of it is yours to decide
and make true in the fact base. The three roads need not lead to the same place.

## Structural constraints (these are requirements, not suggestions)

- A BROAD CAST is the essence of this story. Many distinct people, each of whom KNOWS
  and BELIEVES different things, and several of whom are wrong about what the others
  know. Give the houseful of travellers real, individuated knowledge — not one truth
  and a crowd of bystanders.
- Roughly 22–30 scenes total — enough to give a real cast room, but this is NOT a
  test of length. Keep the spine tight; spend the room on the PEOPLE.
- EXACTLY ONE primary branch point (the fork: NAME / HOLD / ACT) producing 3 distinct
  world-lines, each reaching its OWN distinct terminal ending. (A second, smaller
  downstream fork is allowed but not required.)
- At least two genuine SETUPS that PAY OFF later (planted details that return and
  matter — at least one a piece of knowledge that only one traveller held).
- Decide what is actually true behind the inciting event (the ground truth), and let
  several characters BELIEVE things that differ from the truth and from each other —
  their actions should follow from what THEY believe and what THEY have had a chance
  to learn, not from what you the author know.

Tell the best, most coherent crowded-house mystery you can. Make it hang together:
every consequence should trace to a cause already present, every world-line should
reach a real ending, and every person should know exactly what their own road through
these three nights would let them know — no more, no less.
