## The "story vs pile" verdict (the scale-floor check, verbatim)

- Judge 1: "This is a genuinely authored story, not an internally-consistent pile
  of facts. It has a single clear engine ... and three branches that each take the
  SAME false belief to a DIFFERENT, motivated end."
- Judge 2: "This is a coherent authored story, not a pile of consistent facts."
- Judge 3: "This is a coherent authored story, not a pile of facts. The three
  world-lines share one spine that plants every load-bearing fact before it is
  used."

## Holes flagged (consistent across judges) — completeness blemishes, NOT logic breaks

- **sc-07e (3/3 judges):** the harbourmaster + mainland landing-ledger + recovered
  contraband enter at the resolution with no earlier setup; "a convenient new
  authority rather than a hard causal break" (consistent with gt-kittiwake-greed).
- **sc-07a (2/3 judges, J1+J2):** act_alone's "misreads ... exactly as Crispin
  once did" backreferences the lost-boat backstory established only in the confront
  branch (co-confession, sc-07c) — a cross-branch dependency, "harmless to the
  in-line logic but technically unseeded here" (the in-line cause — no tools +
  failing senses — is present, so the chain holds).
- Judge 3 also noted (minor): in expose, gt-crispin-silent-shame does little work
  because the truth surfaces via the harbourmaster, not Crispin's choice.

## Strongest / weakest (consensus)

- Strongest path: **confront** (3/3 judges, 5/5 across the board — fully
  self-contained, the confession reveal lands where it belongs).
- Strongest single beat: **act_alone**'s tragedy ("the killer he hunted was his own
  certainty" — the planted tools/mercy setup pays off as dramatic irony).
- Weakest: the two late reveals above (expose's harbourmaster, act_alone's
  cross-branch simile) — both completeness, neither a logic break.
