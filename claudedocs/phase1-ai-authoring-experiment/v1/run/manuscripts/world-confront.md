    + gt-dusk-true (gt): At an ordinary dusk Aldous watches Crispin sight the marks with the brass glass and ring the bell on time; every boat comes home safe.
    + gt-maren-edrick (gt): Maren brings fish to the island and speaks of her cousin Edrick, master of the Kittiwake, a boat that 'fishes where she shouldn't' and stays out past the light.

    + gt-early-is-mercy (gt): The early bell is an act of caution and penance: on nights his failing senses cannot trust the true dusk, Crispin rings early to bring every boat in while the channel is sure, accepting a short fishing day over a fatal misread.
    + gt-early-ring (gt): On a fog-thick night Crispin rings the bell a full hour early, while the channel is still certainly passable, and will not say why.
  sc-04 — The tide-ledger [begins=5 ends=0 holding=12]

    + gt-kittiwake-greed (gt): In truth the Kittiwake was lost not to the bell but to Edrick's own greed: he stayed out past the early bell to run contraband and misjudged the channel in the dark.
    + gt-ledger-page (gt): Aldous opens the tide-ledger and finds the early-bell nights marked, and beside one of them the line 'Kittiwake — did not come.'
    + mar-blames-bell (maren): Maren believes the early bell is what killed her cousin Edrick, calling him in wrong and drowning the Kittiwake in the dark.

    + gt-strain-surfaces (gt): At the marks Aldous sees Crispin squint, lean to hear the bar-surf, and grip the brass glass with a shaking hand — the true sign that the old keeper's senses have failed.
