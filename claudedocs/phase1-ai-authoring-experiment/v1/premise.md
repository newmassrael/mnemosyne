# Premise — "The Saltmarsh Bell"

This is the ONLY story input you are given. Everything else — the events, the
characters' secrets, what is really happening, the scene sequence, the branches,
the causality — is yours to invent.

## Setting

A small tidal-island monastery on a marsh coast, late autumn, in a pre-modern
age of sail. Twice a day the channel floods and the island is cut off; at dusk
the community rings a great bronze bell from the tower to guide the fishing
boats home through the shifting marsh channels before the light fails. A boat
that misreads the channel at night does not come back.

## Characters (you may add more)

- **Brother Aldous** — the young bellwright (bell-keeper's apprentice), earnest,
  newly arrived, still learning the tides. The point-of-view character.
- **Father Crispin** — the prior, who has kept the bell for thirty years. Revered
  by the village. Aldous's master.
- **Maren** — a fisherwoman from the mainland village whose family depends on the
  bell; the community's link to the outside.

## The inciting mystery

Aldous discovers that on certain nights Father Crispin rings the bell a full hour
EARLY — before the boats are due, while the channel is still passable. And on at
least one of those early-bell nights, a particular boat failed to return.

## The central dramatic question

What does the early bell mean — and what should Aldous do about it? Father Crispin
will not explain. Aldous can EXPOSE him (carry word to the mainland / the
village), CONFRONT him directly, or ACT ALONE (take the bell into his own hands).
What is really happening behind the early bell is for YOU to decide and make
true in the fact base — and the three responses need not lead to the same place.

## Structural constraints (these are requirements, not suggestions)

- Roughly 8–14 scenes total.
- EXACTLY ONE primary branch point (fork) that produces 2 OR 3 distinct
  world-lines, each reaching its OWN distinct terminal ending. (A second, smaller
  downstream fork is allowed but not required.)
- At least one genuine SETUP that PAYS OFF later (a planted detail that returns
  and matters).
- Decide what is actually true behind the early bell (the ground truth) and let
  at least one character BELIEVE something different from the truth at some point
  — their actions should follow from what they believe, not from what you the
  author know. (Use a separate belief frame for this if it helps.)

Tell the best, most coherent branching story you can. Make it hang together: every
consequence should have a cause already present, every world-line should reach a
real ending, nothing important should dangle.
