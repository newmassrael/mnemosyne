## Honest residue (not downplayed)

- **The literal naming leap is intrinsically off-page.** The deepest honest
  finding: you cannot SHOW a NAMED identification of a villain whose name you are
  WITHHOLDING — the two requirements directly conflict. So "fully closing D1" in
  the literal sense (every step on-page) is incompatible with the mystery itself;
  the achievable and correct close is "show the investigation, reveal the name at
  the gate." v3 reaches that, and the judges reward it. This is a sharper, truer
  statement of the residue than R564's "needs a flashback" — a flashback that
  named Vane would have LEAKED him.
- **The prologue rides the edge.** Judge-2 (the lone knowledge-realism dissent)
  noted sc-00c "steers suspicion toward the shrine before the party earns it" by
  pointing the trail at the holy quarter — a tint, not a leak (the gate confirms
  no name re-extracts early), but the real craft cost of pointing the trail
  anywhere. A shorter trail would lower the tint but weaken the dramatic irony
  the other two judges prized: a genuine dial, not a bug.
- **A slower first hook** (j1, j3): the prologue trades a cold-open for backstory;
  3-0 keep-it, but "only flaw is length" / "slower first hook" is the real pacing
  cost, accepted by the judges, named here.

The real expansion was worth roughly what R565 priced (a small new-authoring
