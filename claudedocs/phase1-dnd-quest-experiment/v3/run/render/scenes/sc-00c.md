## sc-00c — PROLOGUE — Coombe follows the trail toward the upper town

When its work at the sluice was done the robed figure did not go deeper. That told Coombe something on its own — a thing of the deep would have gone down to the deep, back to the black water it belonged in. This one climbed. It took up its covered light and went *up*, out of the lower galleries by a way that Coombe, for all his weeks down here, had not found — a way that bent its slow course toward the upper reaches of the hold and the town above them.

So he followed. That was the hunt and he knew how to do it; it was the one thing in all of this he was sure of his ground on. He gave the light a long lead and went after it soft, keeping a turning between them, reading the trail where he could not see the maker of it — a wet print fading on dry stone, a smear where a robe-hem had dragged a wet wall, the faint far swing of a covered lamp seen around two corners at once and then not. The way climbed. He felt it climbing in his knees and in the slow thinning of the water from his ankles to nothing, and he felt where it was *going* before he could have named the turnings. Up. Toward the upper ways. Toward the town's own roots, and above those, if a man kept on — the upper town, and the quarter where the ground was holy.

And somewhere on that climb the last of his doubt went out of him, and a colder certainty came in to take its place. This was no thing of the deep. He had known it at the sluice and he knew it past all arguing now, watching it climb toward the living world by a living road: the hand that fed the dead to the water was *someone of the town*. One of them. A neighbour. A face he very likely knew, that had nodded to him in the market or taken his coin or wished him well going down — going down, even, after the very work this one did by night. The thought turned his stomach worse than any drowned face had. Not a monster. A *man* of the Reach, or a woman, climbing home from feeding corpses to the flood as easy as climbing home from any night's labour.

He pushed it then — too hard, he'd think after, if he had the leisure to think after. The trail thinned where the ways branched and branched again near the top, old dwarrow passages crossing newer cellar-cuts, and the covered light went around one corner too many and did not show around the next. He cast for it. He went up one branch and back and up another. A dripping somewhere, a draught from somewhere, three ways that all climbed toward the upper town and no telling which one had taken his quarry up into the lamplit lanes above. He had the *deed*, whole and witnessed. He had where it climbed to. He had everything but the last thing — the turn of a face, the say of a name — and in the branching dark near the top of the under-ways, a hand's breadth from the having of it, the trail ran out under his feet and left him alone.
