## sc-00d — PROLOGUE — cut off in the under-ways, the last entry

He sat down where the trail had failed him, in a dry-enough niche off the climbing way, and he got out the journal and the stub of pencil and he wrote it all down while it was whole in him, because a hunter who loses the track keeps the sign, so the next man can take it up. He wrote it careful and he wrote it plain. The fresh dead in the lower galleries, too new for the hold's old drowned. The sluice down the side-gallery where the new ones went in. The robed figure that came by night and carried the dead in its own arms and tipped them to the deep. The trail that climbed — that bent up and up toward the upper town, toward the holy quarter above, a living road for a living hand. He set down everything he was sure of, and the one thing he was *not* sure of he left a clean blank for, because he would not write down a guess as if it were a knowing. He could not name the hand. He had not seen the face. He wrote it as far as he had hunted it and no step further, and where the name should go he wrote only that the figure was someone of the town, and that he had lost it in the upper ways before he could put a face to it.

Done with the writing, he meant to climb. That was the whole of his plan and it was a good one — go up now, at once, while it was hot in him, and lay the tracking before the reeve and the town: that the rising was no curse blown up out of the deep but a thing *fed*, deliberately, by a living hand among them, and that the hand climbed home each night by the upper ways. Let the town hunt from there with more men than one. He wrapped the journal close in its oilskin and put it deep inside his coat, against his chest, the way you keep the one thing that matters dry, and he got his legs under him to climb.

He never climbed. Whether the water had risen behind him while he watched and waited and wrote, or whether the branching ways had turned him without his knowing toward the deep instead of the day, he came down off his niche into a passage that was filling — and then into one that was full — and the under-ways that had let him in did not let him back out. The flood was patient and he was only a man and the dark was very large. It cut him off low down, far from the lantern-light he'd promised himself, and it closed over him there, and the warning he'd meant to carry up went down with him into the cold instead. The journal stayed dry a while longer against his chest, the oilskin keeping its small promise after the man could keep none of his.

His last lines, written before ever he tried to climb, told where he reckoned his body would come to lie, so a finder might know the dead man for who he was. And the very last of them were not for himself at all. *I have got it near enough to touch and could not close my hand on it,* the writing ran, near enough. *Whoever finds this — finish the tracking I could not. The dead are fed by a living hand of this town. Climb where I climbed. Put the name to it I could not put. Name the hand that feeds the dead, and end it.* And there the careful writing stopped, with a clean space after it where a name was never set down, and the water came on.
