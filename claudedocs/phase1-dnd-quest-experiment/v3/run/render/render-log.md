# Render log — v3 prologue (sc-00a..sc-00d), Coombe's POV

## Scenes rendered

- `scenes/sc-00a.md` — Coombe goes down alone, reads the fresh dead in the floodwater, reasons that a living hand is adding new tithes.
- `scenes/sc-00b.md` — staked out at the sluice, Coombe watches a robed/hooded figure carry a body down by night and tip it in; he knows he has found the hand.
- `scenes/sc-00c.md` — Coombe follows the trail up out of the under-ways as it bends toward the upper town and the holy quarter; certain it is someone of the town, he loses the trail near the top.
- `scenes/sc-00d.md` — the last journal entry: he sets down all he tracked, means to climb and warn the town, but is cut off by the rising flood and drowns, the journal still on him, begging a finder to name the hand.

Approximate prologue total: ~2,400 words (561 / 615 / 581 / 654).

## How I kept the v2 voice

Matched against `v2/run/render/world-claim.md`:

- **Third-person past, deep POV.** Stayed in Coombe's head the way v2 sits inside Doran / Henna / Pip — the narration carries his reasoning and his "read" of the world (the skinner's eye for a fresh kill vs. a week-old one mirrors Doran reading footing and Pip reading a locked drawer).
- **Em-dash asides.** The hallmark v2 cadence — a clause cut in with em-dashes mid-sentence ("— and not long ago —", "— at prayer, or only catching its breath —", "— too hard, he'd think after —"). Used throughout, not over-thick.
- **Folk register / plain idiom.** "the long and short of," "ran it to ground," "put a face to it," "no business be any," "on my hand," echoing v2's "face like a gate-post," "buried friends," "did the sum fast."
- **Italics for the turning word.** v2 leans on a single italicised word at the pivot (*held*, *kept*, *will*, *recent*). Reused sparingly: *not*, *brought*, *now*, *held*, *person*, *someone of the town*, *deed*, the closing journal italics.
- **Long comma-stitched sentences opening a scene, shorter beats at the gut-punch.** Followed the v2 rhythm: a wide establishing sentence, then a one-line hammer ("And then, by night, somebody came down to the sluice." / "He never climbed.").
- **Continuity of facts.** Tied to the established world: Maeve's tithe-lore, the lantern-boy / big lamp, the third-night promise, the oilskin-wrapped journal kept dry against the chest (which v2 sc-13 later discovers verbatim), the careful-writer characterisation that v2 sc-13/sc-14 pay off. Coombe = "Tam's master," the last delver, consistent with sc-05 and sc-13.

## How I kept the figure unnamed (the hard rule)

- The figure is **only ever** "the robed figure," "the hooded shape," "someone of the town," "a living hand," "a neighbour," "a face he very likely knew" — never a name, role, or face.
- **Face never shown:** explicitly "the face of it lost in the hood and turned away besides"; in the journal Coombe "had not seen the face" and "left a clean blank" where the name would go.
- **Never tied to the shrine by name.** I did not use the word *shrine* at all. The trail's destination is rendered as geography only — "the quarter where the ground was holy," "the holy quarter above" — so the reader gets the upward bend toward the upper town WITHOUT a place-name that fingers the shrine. No "shrine quarter" literal string, no "keeper," no "brother," no "Vane."
- **Trail kept short of the reveal.** Coombe loses the trail in the branching upper ways "a hand's breadth from the having of it," then drowns before climbing. The deed is fully witnessed (bodies fed to the sluice) and the hunter gets close (someone of the town, climbing toward the upper/holy quarter), but identity is left an explicit blank — the journal even ends "with a clean space after it where a name was never set down."
- **Verification:** `grep -ni -E "vane|shrine|keeper|brother|priest"` over all four scenes returns NONE. Only Coombe and the narration are present (prologue is before the party).
