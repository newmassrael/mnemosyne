# Quest briefing — "The Drowned Hold of Vael Mooren"

A player-facing map of the adventure's quests: what each asks, where you pick it
up, what it needs done first, who leads it, and how it can turn out. The
adventure has one shared opening and then one hard choice at the deep vault
(scene 22) that splits it into three roads — SHATTER, CLAIM, PARLEY — each with
a different ending.

## The quests

**Still the Rising** (the main quest)
- Objective: go down into Vael Mooren, find what wakes the drowned dead, and end it.
- Taken up: the reeve's hall, at the start (scene sc-02).
- Led by: the fighter.
- Needs first: **the Warden's Key** — the deep vault cannot be opened, and the
  crown cannot be reached, until the key is recovered.
- Outcome: completed on ALL THREE roads — but what "ending the rising" means
  differs by road (the crown shattered, claimed, or bound by oath).

**The Warden's Key** (side quest — the gate)
- Objective: recover the drowned warden's own key; nothing else opens the vault.
- Taken up: the locked vault door, deep in the hold (scene sc-16).
- Led by: the rogue.
- Needs first: nothing.
- Outcome: recovered on the way down (sc-17), and the vault grinds open (sc-19) —
  this happens on every road, before the fork. It is the real lock on the main
  quest: no key, no crown.

**The Last Delver** (side quest — skippable)
- Objective: find the delver who vanished, and bring word back to his apprentice.
- Taken up: the lantern-house in town (scene sc-05), from the boy.
- Led by: the rogue.
- Needs first: nothing.
- Outcome: finished on SHATTER and PARLEY (the party finds the body and journal
  and tells the boy). **Left OPEN on CLAIM** — a party that takes the crown and
  turns to ruling the Reach never goes back to tell the boy. A real loose end you
  can choose to leave.

**The Reliquary** (side quest — a trap)
- Objective: bring the shrine-keeper the crown, so he may "seal it safely."
- Taken up: the shrine in town (scene sc-07), from the keeper.
- Led by: the cleric.
- Needs first: nothing — but it can only be FULFILLED if the party comes out of
  the vault holding the crown.
- Outcome: completed **only on CLAIM** — and the moment the party hands the crown
  over, the keeper turns on them (the errand was bait). **Left OPEN on SHATTER and
  PARLEY** — there is no crown to give (it was unmade, or left with the warden),
  so the trap never springs.

## The fork at the deep vault (scene sc-22)

Once the party reaches the warden's shade in the deep vault — having opened it
with the key — they choose:

- **SHATTER** — the wizard unmakes the crown. The rising ends, the warden is
  freed into death, the hold's wealth is lost, and the rival sellsword, cheated
  of the prize, turns on the party.
- **CLAIM** — the party takes the crown's power. The reliquary trap can now be
  sprung (hand it to the keeper — and be betrayed). The Reach falls under the
  crown-holder's rule. The delver's boy is never told. A dark road.
- **PARLEY** — the cleric binds the rising by oath with the warden's shade. The
  warden keeps the crown but stills the dead; the town must keep an eternal
  tithe-watch. A bittersweet road.

## The buried truths (earned, not told at the start)

- The rising is no outside curse: the **shrine-keeper himself** has been feeding
  corpse-tithes to wake the warden, meaning to rule the Reach. (Learned at sc-14.)
- The warden's shade **can be reasoned with** — he keeps the crown under duty,
  not malice, and would hear a fair oath. (Learned at sc-18 — the door to PARLEY.)
- The **rival sellsword's** loyalty is only until the crown is in reach; his
  betrayal is coming. (Surfaces after the fork, on each road.)
