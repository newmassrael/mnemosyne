# Judge brief (A/B) — does the longer version earn its length?

You are an experienced tabletop-adventure reviewer. You will be given TWO
versions of the same dungeon-delve road — arm X and arm Y — plus a player-facing
quest briefing. One is longer (it opens with a prologue following a doomed
investigator before the main party arrives); the other begins with the party.
Judge them as games, axis by axis, and decide whether the longer opening is worth
it.

Read EXACTLY these and NO other file under `claudedocs/phase1-dnd-quest-experiment/`:
- this brief
- the quest briefing you are given
- arm X prose (path given)
- arm Y prose (path given)

You do NOT know how either was made, which is longer-by-design, or what is being
tested. Judge only what is in front of you.

## For EACH arm, score 1-5 (cite sc-NN):

- **Knowledge realism** — does each person know only what they could come by?
  In particular: when the party names who is behind the rising, has the story
  laid a path to that knowledge the reader has SEEN, or does it arrive as a
  conclusion from a found document? Is the villain's identity revealed at a point
  that feels earned (not spoiled early, not asserted from nowhere)?
- **Opening / pacing** — does the opening earn its length and pull you in, or
  does it delay the hook? If one arm opens with a prologue, does that prologue add
  real tension/grounding, or is it backstory you would cut?
- **Dramatic tension** — does the story's handling of what-the-reader-knows
  versus what-the-characters-know work? (Knowing a thing the party doesn't can
  build dread; knowing too much can deflate a mystery.)
- **Completion / polish** — does it read as a finished scenario a table could run?
- **Overall (1-5).**

## Forced A/B calls (X or Y, one line each):

- Which arm has the stronger **knowledge realism** (the villain's identification
  feels most earned and shown)?
- Which is the more **finished overall**?
- **Keep-the-prologue call:** one arm opens with an investigator's prologue. Knowing
  both, would you KEEP that prologue or CUT it? Say which arm has it and your call.
- **Seam spot-check:** name any scene in either arm where a character knows
  something the text gave them no path to — or where a secret is spoiled too early.

## Deliverable

Write `judge-<n>.md` in the directory you are told, with both arms' five scores,
the four forced calls, and a closing sentence. Reply with a short summary of your
forced calls and the two overall scores.
