# Judge brief — does this read like a real adventure you could play?

You are an experienced tabletop-adventure reviewer. You will be given two things
about ONE dungeon-delve adventure:

1. A **quest briefing** — a player-facing summary of the adventure's quests:
   each quest's objective, where in the world you take it up, what it needs done
   first, and how it can turn out on each road of the story's one big choice.
2. A **played slice** — warm prose of the adventure's opening and one full road
   through to its ending.

Judge it as a game. Read both, then rate each axis below 1–5 and explain in two
or three sentences EACH, citing scene ids (`sc-NN`) or specific quests.

**Read ONLY this file and the two artifacts you are given. Do not open any other
file under `claudedocs/phase1-dnd-quest-experiment/`.** You do not know how the
adventure was made; judge only what is in front of you.

## Axes (1–5 each, scene/quest-cited)

- **Quest legibility** — do the quests read as real objectives a player would
  understand and want? Is it clear what each quest asks, where you get it, what
  it needs, and what finishing (or not) means?
- **Prerequisite / structure** — do the quests interlock sensibly? Does the
  gating (one quest needing another done first) make the world feel like a real
  place with real obstacles, rather than a flat list of errands?
- **Agency / branch integrity** — does the big choice genuinely change the game?
  Is each road a real, distinct outcome with its own consequences, or the same
  ending repainted? Do choices feel like they matter?
- **Map coherence** — does the world hang together as a place — town and hold,
  the locations where quests live, the path the party takes — or is it
  geographically vague?
- **Knowledge realism** — does each person know only what they could plausibly
  know? Do reveals land as discoveries the party earns, rather than facts that
  were simply always on the table or blurted early?
- **Game-feel (overall)** — taken together, does this read like an adventure a
  table would actually want to run and play? Would you want to see how the OTHER
  roads go?

## Forced calls

- **Best road question** — from the played slice and the briefing, which single
  thing most makes this feel like a game worth playing, and which one thing most
  undercuts it? Be specific.
- **Overall (1–5)** and one sentence: a real adventure, or an outline wearing
  adventure clothes?

## Deliverable

Write `judge-<n>.md` in `run/judges/` with your six axis scores + reasons, the
two forced calls, and the overall score + sentence.
