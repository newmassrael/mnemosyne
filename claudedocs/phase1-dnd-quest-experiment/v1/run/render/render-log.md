# Render log — world-claim

## Voice choices

- **Doran (fighter)** — plain, blunt, soldier's pragmatism. Reads terrain and
  people as threats/footing. Carries the crown at the end because no one else
  reaches for it. His register is short clauses and dry sums ("no curse in it,
  only work").
- **Mother Henna (cleric)** — warm, devout, attuned to the spirit. She is the
  reader of the leash and the weariness; she is the one with a sense for
  falseness, which I leaned on deliberately so her *failure* to sense Vane's
  falseness (sc-04) lands as honest, not stupid. Her arc is the tragedy of the
  claim road: she carries an unspoken oath to Orrek that dies in her throat at
  sc-23c, and she keeps Vane's errand in good faith straight into sc-28c.
- **Pip (rogue)** — quick, watchful, "the last out of any room." She finds where
  the others fight. I gave her the overheard incense line (sc-06), the secret
  stair (sc-10), the journal/body (sc-13), and the key (sc-17) as the outline
  assigns, and kept her competence quiet rather than flashy.
- **Lysa (wizard)** — precise, scholarly, the only rune-reader. She assembles
  facts cold and certain. I used her as the deliverer of the journal's naming
  (sc-14) and the three roads / Orrek's nature (sc-15, sc-18).
- **NPCs** — Reeve Calder gruff and literal ("a curse"); Brother Vane warm and
  smooth, his menace kept entirely subtextual until sc-29c; Tam anxious and
  willed-hopeful; Old Maeve cryptic, long-form, comfortless; Skell hard and
  transactional; Orrek's shade weary and formal, never wrathful.

## Withheld truths — how they were held

- **f-004 (Vane fed the corpse-tithes), withhold first_at=sc-14.** Through
  sc-01 to sc-13 the narration and every character treat Vane as a genuine
  friend. sc-04 and sc-07 are written from inside the party's trust; the prose
  hints only at *something concealed* without naming it ("no kindness behind
  it... but not a word of that was in the room"), which I read as permitted
  dramatic irony in the `gt` narration without voicing the withheld content —
  the *fact itself* (he fed the tithes) is never stated until sc-14. The sc-06
  incense overhear and Maeve's "someone living is feeding it" are deliberately
  un-attributed clues, not the named secret. First explicit naming is sc-14,
  on the journal, as required.
- **f-101 (Skell will turn the moment the crown is in reach), withhold
  first_at=sc-26c.** At sc-11 and sc-21 Skell is read only as a man who *might*
  turn (Doran's general read of the type) and as a man fixed on the crown's
  worth — both openly-stated `state` facts. His actual betraying intent /
  resolution is not voiced until sc-26c, where on the claim road it resolves
  not as a betrayal but as his pragmatic submission. Held until its scene.
- **f-171 (Orrek can be reasoned with), withhold first_at=sc-18.** Henna senses
  at sc-12 only that a *will* could be "spoken to" — a general theological read,
  not knowledge of Orrek specifically. The concrete fact that Orrek's shade
  would hear a fair oath is first stated at sc-18 (its own scene), via Lysa's
  reading, as required.

## Ambiguities noted

- The outline header says "outside order=20," and the scene IDs jump from the
  trunk (sc-01..sc-22) to the claim branch (sc-23c..sc-32c). I treated the
  given top-to-bottom order as the road's order and rendered straight through.
- The CLAIM road is a tragedy-of-success: the reeve's main charge (end the
  rising) is technically discharged (sc-25c, "in a dark coin") even though the
  outcome is grim. I let the *charge* land as discharged in the fiction while
  letting the *cost* land emotionally, per the facts' own framing.
- Two errands are left deliberately unpaid on this road and I honored that: the
  lantern-boy's errand (Tam never told of his master — sc-31c/32c) and Henna's
  oath-option (never spoken — superseded by the claim choice at sc-22/23c). I
  did not invent closure the outline withholds.
- f-191 superseded by f-401 (sc-23c) and f-401 superseded by f-410 (sc-28c):
  rendered as the crown physically changing hands warden→party→Vane, so each
  supersession is an in-fiction event rather than a restatement.
