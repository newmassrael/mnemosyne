## sc-30c — The crown changes hands — who rules the Reach

This was the end their grasping had bought them, and it had a name, and the name was Brother Vane. They had not been beaten in the deep vault. They had won there — taken the crown off the warden's brow, brought the wild dead to heel, mastered the very rising the reeve had charged them to end. And then they had carried their winning up the long stairs in their own hands and laid it, warm and trusting, into the one waiting palm that had wanted it all along. The crown ruled Karrow's Reach now, as it was always going to; the only thing the party had decided, down all those stairs, was *whose brow it ruled from*. They had reached for it. They had got it. And in the getting they had delivered it — and the Reach — to the schemer who had sent them down after it, the friend who had blessed their bowed heads and then turned their own dead on them the instant the iron was his. The living of the Reach were held by the dead, and the dead answered to Brother Vane, because four delvers had grasped at a crown and handed it to him.

Up in his stone hall Reeve Calder learned what his authority was worth now, which was nothing. His charge had been to end the rising, and the rising was ended — mastered, *kept*, the dead taught a new hand — and the new hand was not his, and would never be his. His hall held no power at all over the man who wore the iron and the corpses that came at its call. The Reach answered to the crown. The crown answered to Brother Vane. And Calder, who had hired four delvers to save his town and got it stolen out from under him by the brother on the hill, sat in a hall that no longer meant anything, in a Reach that had only changed masters, and learned the difference too late to spend it.
