# Judge 1 — A/B verdict: "The Drowned Hold of Vael Mooren" (CLAIM road)

Both arms run the CLAIM fork and are scene-for-scene identical through sc-12,
sharing all setup, characterization, and prose. The divergences cluster in the
knowledge-chain scenes (sc-05, sc-13, sc-14, sc-18) and the betrayal ending
(sc-29c, sc-30c). I judged only those seams and their downstream effect.

## Arm X

**Knowledge realism — 5.**
Arm X lays a complete, visible path to every piece of knowledge. The boy at
sc-05 plants the investigation himself — his master "went down to follow where
the new ones came from... somebody was *putting* them down there... and he
meant to learn who" — so the journal's later content has a seeded origin. Pip
carries Maeve's incense/"shrine-work" overhear (sc-06) and surfaces it at the
body (sc-13: "Incense, she said. Said it smelled like shrine-work"). The journal
at sc-14 *shows* the tracking ("a watch kept, a path followed, the source of the
corpse-tithes run down at last to the one who fed them") rather than asserting a
conclusion. Crucially, sc-14 explicitly assembles three independent witnesses —
Maeve's living-hand lore, Pip's shrine-smell, the dead man's hunt — into one
verdict. No one knows anything the text did not earn.

**Agency / branch integrity — 5.**
The ending commits without hedging. sc-29c fully dramatizes the betrayal: the
leash visibly changes hands, the dead turn on the party, Vane gloats that they
"set it in his palm." sc-30c names the outcome as chosen, not fated — "the name
was Brother Vane... the only thing the party had decided, down all those stairs,
was *whose brow it ruled from*. They had reached for it. They had got it." The
CLAIM road's signature loose ends are honored: the reliquary trap springs
(sc-28c→29c) and the delver's boy is left untold (sc-31c, sc-32c). The road
feels earned by the grasp at the crown.

**Completion / polish — 5.**
Reads as a runnable, finished scenario. The three-roads framing (sc-18: "unmake
it, claim it, or bind it by an oath") matches the warden's own "three ends"
(sc-20) with no internal contradiction. Each clue is placed and paid off.

**Overall — 5.**

## Arm Y

**Knowledge realism — 3.**
Arm Y compresses the knowledge chain and breaks one link. At sc-05 the boy
gives NO hint of his master's corpse-tithe investigation — only that he went
down twelve nights ago. Yet sc-14 credits the discovery to "the dead boy had
named at last" / "the dead boy named" — a path the text never laid; the boy
named nothing. The journal's tracking is asserted ("he'd tracked them to their
source, and he'd written down what the source was") rather than shown. Pip's
incense clue is dropped from the body scene (sc-13) and only referenced
secondhand in Lysa's recap (sc-14: "The incense Pip had heard the old woman
mutter") — the corroboration is thinner and the on-page surfacing is gone. The
Vane verdict still arrives, but on a partly-unlaid path.

**Agency / branch integrity — 2.**
sc-30c hedges the ending explicitly, the exact failure the brief flags: "it came
to the same end whichever way you turned it... Whether it had stayed on the
party's brow or... passed to Brother Vane's, the shape of the thing did not
change." This collapses the chosen CLAIM outcome into an inevitability and
strips the party's decision of weight. sc-29c also softens the betrayal — Vane's
gloat is shorter and the party's complicity ("carried it all the way back to
him and set it in his palm") is muted. The loose ends still land (boy untold,
trap sprung), but the ending refuses to commit to what the choice earned.

**Completion / polish — 3.**
Visible seams. sc-18 muddies the choice-accounting: it first names three "ends"
(unmake / claim / *left to him*) and then bolts on the parley as "a fourth
thing," which sits awkwardly against sc-20's "only three ends." Combined with
the dropped clue and the hedged finale, it reads as a strong draft, not a
finished cut.

**Overall — 3.**

## Forced A/B calls

- **Stronger knowledge realism:** **X.** X seeds the investigation at sc-05 and
  surfaces every clue on-page; Y credits the boy with naming Vane (sc-14) on a
  path sc-05 never laid.
- **Stronger agency:** **X.** X's sc-30c commits ("whose brow it ruled from");
  Y's sc-30c hedges ("the same end whichever way you turned it").
- **More finished overall:** **X.**
- **Seam spot-check:** **Arm Y, sc-14** — Lysa's recap and narration credit "the
  dead boy [who] named at last" the shrine-keeper, but Arm Y's sc-05 has the boy
  reveal nothing about his master's tithe-investigation, so there is no path to
  that knowledge. (Arm X closes this seam at sc-05 by having the boy state the
  investigation outright.) No comparable seam found in Arm X.

## What separates them

Same adventure, but Arm X earns its knowledge and commits to its ending where
Arm Y asserts one and hedges the other: X plants the boy's investigation (sc-05)
and surfaces every clue, then names the CLAIM outcome as chosen (sc-30c); Y
drops the seed yet credits the boy (sc-14 seam) and dissolves the ending into
"the same end whichever way" (sc-30c). X is the more finished, higher-completion
cut.
