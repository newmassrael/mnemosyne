# Repair spec — dnd-quest v2 (the deficiency list)

This is the orchestrator-assembled list of what v1 left deficient, drawn
DETERMINISTICALLY from two independent blind sources in v1: the blind
extractor's `extract-log.md` (the knowledge-acquisition seams) and the three
blind judges' unanimous agency dock (`run/judges/judge-{1,2,3}.md`). It is the
critique the repair works from — nothing here is invented by the repair
lineage; each item cites the v1 evidence.

The v1 base, render, and judge/extract logs live in
`claudedocs/phase1-dnd-quest-experiment/v1/`. The CLAIM road is the one that was
rendered and judged; the repair targets it.

## D1 — the delver's journal names the shrine-keeper without a shown chain (sc-14)

- v1 evidence: extractor flag 1; the rendered sc-14 named Brother Vane as a
  finished conclusion. At the FACT level f-130 already records the delver
  "tracked the corpse-tithes to their source and found the shrine-keeper
  feeding the dead" — so the chain EXISTS in the base but (a) was rendered as a
  flat naming and (b) is asserted with no earlier SETUP that it pays off.
- Repair: (author) seed the delver's investigation as an early setup the
  journal pays off — a fact that the last delver went down TRACKING the tithes
  (this is also why the lantern-boy's errand exists); make f-130 / the journal
  read-out pay it off so the naming is EARNED, not bare. (render) re-render
  sc-14 so the journal reads as the delver's recorded investigation reaching
  Vane, not a verdict dropped in.

## D2 — Pip's incense clue is overheard, never shared, yet the case uses it (sc-06 -> sc-14)

- v1 evidence: extractor flag 2. f-052 (sc-06, frame rogue): "Pip lingers
  behind ... overhears her mutter the new tithe smells of incense — shrine-work"
  — Pip-only, never shared. The rendered sc-14 had Lysa's synthesis draw on
  "the incense Pip had heard". A planted clue that both (a) is used without a
  hand-off and (b) otherwise just dangles unused.
- Repair: (author) add the HAND-OFF — a fact where Pip tells the party what he
  overheard (turning the dangling private clue into a shared one that
  contributes to the case against Vane), and have the sc-14 deduction
  evidence-cite it. (render) re-render so Lysa's certainty rests on the journal
  + the shared tithe-lore + Pip's now-shared incense clue, with the hand-off
  shown.

## D3 — the keeping's endings are counted inconsistently (sc-18 vs sc-20)

- v1 evidence: extractor flag 3. At sc-18 the wizard frames "three ends
  (unmake / claim / leave) + a fourth: the shade can be reasoned with"; at sc-20
  the warden's shade knows "only three ends — unmaking, claiming, or a sworn
  oath". The oath/parley is a FOURTH in one frame and the THIRD in the other.
- Repair: (author) reconcile to a single consistent count — the three real
  roads are unmake / claim / oath(parley), with "leave it to him" the
  non-option (doing nothing). Amend the wizard's belief facts (f-171 / f-172)
  so the wizard and the warden agree the oath is the third road, not a fourth.

## D4 — the CLAIM ending hedges and flattens its own agency (sc-30c)

- v1 evidence: ALL THREE judges' agency dock (4/5, unanimous), citing sc-30c
  "came to the same end whichever way you turned it" — which flattens CLAIM's
  keep-vs-hand-over sub-choice. The extractor (sc-30c note) confirms the prose
  deliberately declines to pin the ruler. But the CLAIM road as AUTHORED does
  commit: the party HANDS the crown to Vane (f-408/f-409/f-410) and Vane betrays
  and rules (f-411/f-412). The hedge is a RENDER artifact contradicting the
  authored outcome.
- Repair: (render only) re-render sc-29c/sc-30c to COMMIT to the road actually
  authored — the party's grasp for power delivered the Reach to its schemer; a
  specific, earned doom (Vane rules, the party undone by their own choice), not
  "same end whichever way". No structural change: the facts already say Vane
  ends up ruling.
