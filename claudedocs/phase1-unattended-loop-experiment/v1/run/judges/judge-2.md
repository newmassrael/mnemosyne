# Verdict — judge-2 (road: descend)

Read as a reader, alone, only from what is on the page.

## Scores

1. **Coherence — 5.** Every effect has its cause on the page and the causes close
   cleanly: the torn log page (sc-03) is found again folded in Tomas's coat
   (sc-11d), the "gone" rope from the bare hook (sc-06) is the rigged handline she
   descends (sc-09d), and the dead ground the valley swore was empty (sc-05) is the
   real cold fire ring Sella fed for two days (sc-10d). No event happens without a
   thread already laid for it.

2. **Knowledge realism — 5.** Nobody knows more than they could. Wren reasons her
   way to Tomas's mind from physical evidence — "You don't bring rope to a
   wildfire. You bring rope to a person" (sc-06) — rather than being told; the
   dispatcher only reports what instruments show; and Sella's two-days backstory is
   explicitly flagged as "the woman would tell her later" (sc-10d), not magically
   known in the moment. Wren even polices her own inference — "Only missing. She
   had not earned any darker word than that" (sc-05).

3. **Choice & consequence — 5.** The hold-vs-descend choice is given real weight on
   both sides — "she was the tower now. If she went down the draw she took the
   district's only eyes down with her" (sc-07) — and the descend road earns an
   outcome the choice uniquely produces: because she went down, she puts a hand to
   the ash, finds Sella alive, and finds Tomas's body (sc-10d/sc-11d). This is a
   genuine consequence of going, not the same beat relabeled.

4. **Quest legibility — 5.** The charge is stated plainly — confirm the west-draw
   smoke, act on the disappearance before the window closes (sc-04) — and its
   completion is unambiguous: the fire is confirmed real, Tomas is accounted for,
   Sella is rescued, and Wren radios the whole outcome out at dawn, "giving the
   valley everything the night had cost" (sc-12d). Achieved, and achieved *because*
   she descended.

5. **Overall game-feel — 4.** Taken whole it is a tight, satisfying world-line I'd
   want to finish and would want the other road of — the branch point is legible
   and the payoff lands. It reads a hair more as polished short story than as
   played game, and one plot device (the torn page) is doing hook-work slightly
   ahead of character logic (see weakest link), which keeps it just short of
   clockwork.

## Weakest link

**The torn last page (sc-03).** It's the one beat where a device outruns
character logic: a careful, by-the-book lookout tearing the official close-of-watch
record out of his own logbook "in a hurry" is the least plausible action on the
page — a man in a hurry to go down a draw would simply leave the log and go. The
tear exists mainly to plant the "missing last word" mystery. The story does pay it
off honestly (the page is in his coat, sc-11d), so it's a strain rather than a gap,
but it's the single seam a reader can see the author's hand through.

## Real game?

Yes — a coherent, self-consistent small game whose central choice produces a
real, earned consequence on this road.
