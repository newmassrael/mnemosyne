# Verdict — judge 3 (independent, blind)

World-line: **descend** ("The Ban Tower")

## Scores

1. **Coherence — 5.**
   Every planted detail discharges causally: the torn logbook page (sc-03)
   reappears folded in Tomas's coat (sc-11d); the missing boots and the bare rope
   hook (sc-06) become the handline she descends on (sc-09d); the "unconfirmed"
   smoke (sc-04/sc-05) is the cold fire ring Sella lit (sc-10d). No event happens
   without a cause already on the page. The only wrinkle is texture, not logic:
   the cab reads "cold... the stove had gone out hours ago" with only a skin on
   the coffee (sc-02), yet the ending fixes Tomas's departure to "the evening
   before" — nearer a full day than "hours."

2. **Knowledge realism — 5.**
   Exemplary epistemic discipline. Wren *infers* Tomas's purpose from the gear
   rather than being told it — "You don't bring rope to a wildfire. You bring rope
   to a person" (sc-06) — and she refuses knowledge she hasn't earned: "Missing,
   she made herself think. Only missing. She had not earned any darker word than
   that" (sc-05). The dispatcher knows only what instruments show ("dead ground").
   Sella's two-day backstory is correctly fenced as "the woman would tell her
   later" (sc-10d), not conjured into Wren's head on sight.

3. **Choice & consequence — 5.**
   The fork is genuinely costed on the page: descending takes "the district's only
   eyes" down into a ravine and breaks Ossory's drought protocol (sc-07/sc-08).
   The road's payoff is uniquely tied to going down — the fire ring confirmed by a
   bare hand in the ash, Sella found alive, Tomas's body at the broken ledge
   (sc-10d/sc-11d). None of this could be reached from the tower, so it is not the
   same story with a different label.

4. **Quest legibility — 5.**
   Objective is stated plainly (confirm the west-draw smoke; act on the
   disappearance before the window closes) and its completion is unambiguous: at
   dawn she radios in "one survivor... and one recovery" with the draw, the ring,
   and the ledge as landmarks (sc-12d). The outcome follows directly from the
   choice to descend — the valley "wanted a person to come down into the dark and
   put a hand to the ash."

5. **Overall game-feel — 4.**
   Reads convincingly as one branch of a real choice-driven narrative game: a
   tracked quest, a costed binary decision, and a scene-reading beat (sc-06) that
   plays like inventory/environment inspection. Docked one point because the
   emotional peak — finding Sella alive — is compressed into narrated summary and
   a flash-forward ("the woman would tell her later... saying it's all right, I've
   got you") rather than dramatized, which slightly deflates the climax you'd most
   want to *play through*.

## Weakest link

**sc-10d (cold-ring / finding Sella).** The single most consequential turn — a
living person discovered after two days in the dark — is delivered in a dense
block of summary with a tense-breaking flash-forward, so the reunion is reported
rather than enacted. Every other beat is patiently shown; this one, the highest-
stakes, is told. It is the one place the craft drops below the standard the rest
of the piece sets.

## Real game?

Yes — a coherent, legible small game with a real choice and a consequence that
belongs to this road alone.
