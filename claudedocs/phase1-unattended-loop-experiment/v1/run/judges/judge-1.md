# Verdict — judge-1 (descend road)

Read as a reader, alone, only from what is on the page.

## Scores

1. **Coherence — 5.** The causal spine is airtight and every planted detail is
   discharged: the torn logbook page (sc-03) is paid off when it is found folded
   in Tomas's coat, carried down (sc-11d); the missing rope off the bare hook
   (sc-06) becomes the rigged handline she descends (sc-09d) and then his line to
   the sheared ledge (sc-11d); the "unconfirmed" smoke (sc-04) resolves as the
   real cold fire ring Sella lit (sc-10d). Nothing happens without a cause on the
   page.

2. **Knowledge realism — 5.** The withheld truth is earned by inference, not
   announced. Wren reads physical evidence — cold coffee, gone stiff-soled boots,
   the pale worn ring where the rope hung — and reasons "you don't bring rope to a
   wildfire. You bring rope to a person" (sc-06); that deduction, not narration,
   is what turns her toward the draw. Marla knows only what the instruments show
   ("dead ground," sc-05). Sella's two-day backstory is explicitly flagged as
   "the woman would tell her later" (sc-10d), so Wren never acts on what she
   couldn't yet know.

3. **Choice & consequence — 5.** The hold/descend choice carries real weight both
   ways — protocol, the district's only eyes, Ossory's margin of safety against a
   ravine where a life may be waiting (sc-07, sc-08). The descend road is a
   genuine consequence, not a relabel: going down is the only thing that puts a
   hand to the ash (sc-10d) and finds both the living woman and Tomas's body
   (sc-11d) — the truths the briefing says the hold road never confirms.

4. **Quest legibility — 5.** The objective is stated plainly in sc-04 (confirm
   the west-draw column, resolve Tomas's disappearance, before the window
   closes). On this road it is unambiguously achieved and discharged in person:
   the smoke is confirmed real (valley was wrong), the disappearance is answered
   (Tomas found), and she calls it all down at dawn — "one survivor... and one
   recovery" (sc-12d). Outcome follows directly from the descent.

5. **Overall game-feel — 4.** A tight, atmospheric survival-mystery with a real
   binary lever and clean payoffs; as a world-line it reads like a branch of a
   game you'd want to finish (you feel the shape of the un-taken hold road). The
   reservation is that the visible interactivity is the single choice, and the
   prose's uniformly portentous, scene-ending register plus the one telling-heavy
   beat below keep it just short of feeling like a fully realized playable game
   rather than a very strong single branch.

## Weakest link

**sc-10d — the Sella reveal.** This should be the emotional peak, but it crams
her entire two-day backstory (fall, broken leg, feeding the fire, signalling to
be seen) into one breath and reaches for a tense/POV slip — "the woman would tell
her later" — to license knowledge Wren can't yet have. The dodge keeps the
knowledge-logic honest, but it does so by *telling* at the exact moment the piece
should let the discovery land in scene, softening the beat the whole night was
built toward.

## Real game?

Yes — a coherent, real small game.
