# Brief — judge (fresh context, independent)

You are reading one world-line of a short branching game, told as prose, plus a
one-page briefing of the game's quest and choice. Judge it as a reader. You are
alone; there are no other judges to defer to. Answer only from what is on the
page.

## Read

1. `quest-briefing.md` — the game's objective, its central choice, and how this
   world-line resolves it.
2. `world-<road>.md` — the rendered world-line.

## Score each axis 1–5 (5 = best) with a one-line reason citing a scene

1. **Coherence** — does the story hold together causally? Do events follow from
   what came before, or are there gaps where something happens with no cause on
   the page?
2. **Knowledge realism** — does each character seem to know only what they could
   plausibly know? Does anyone act on information they were never shown? Is the
   withheld truth earned by inference rather than simply announced?
3. **Choice & consequence** — does the central choice feel real, and does this
   world-line feel like a genuine consequence of it (not the same story with a
   different label)?
4. **Quest legibility** — is the objective clear, is it clear whether it was
   achieved on this road, and did that outcome follow from the choice?
5. **Overall game-feel** — taken whole, does this read like a real, playable
   small game you'd want to finish?

## Then

- **Weakest link**: name the single scene or beat that most hurt the piece, and
  why. Be specific and honest — a flaw named is worth more than praise.
- **Real game?**: one line — is this a coherent, real small game, yes or no?

You are BLIND to how this was authored, to any other version, and to what the
result will be used for. Judge only what you read.
