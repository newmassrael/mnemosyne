# Premise — "The Ban Tower" (fresh, IP-free)

A neutral seed for a SMALL branching game. This file fixes the SETTING and the
required STRUCTURAL SHAPE; it does NOT author the facts — the facts, scenes,
frames, quest, fork, and withheld secret are yours to invent, subject only to
the shape below. Nothing here is drawn from any existing work.

## Setting

A lone fire-watch tower — the **Ban Tower** — on a ridge above a dry valley at
the end of a long drought. A relief lookout rides up to take the season's last
shift and finds the tower empty: the senior lookout who should be handing over
is gone, the fire-log has a torn-out page, and the last radioed entry reports a
smoke column in the **west draw** that the valley dispatcher never confirmed and
no one else saw. The relief lookout has one night and a hand-crank radio.

## Required structural shape (the game must instantiate ALL of these)

Author whatever concrete story you like, but the finished store MUST contain:

1. **Scale** — 14–20 scenes; a small cast of **4–6 characters**, each its own
   epistemic frame; a `ground-truth` frame for what actually happened.
2. **One fork** — a single moral/agency choice partway through (roughly the
   midpoint) that splits into **exactly two world-lines** (two branches), each
   with its own aftermath scenes. Both endings are canonical.
3. **A withheld secret** — one load-bearing truth (what really became of the
   senior lookout, and whether the west-draw smoke was real) that a reader of
   the default telling is NOT told outright — it is reconstructed. Encode it as
   a disclosure plan whose default withholds, revealed (if at all) only late and
   only on the world-line where it comes out.
4. **One quest** — a tracked objective the relief lookout pursues (e.g. confirm
   the fire / find the senior lookout), encoded per the quest convention, with a
   giving setup and a per-road completion (done on one branch, open/unresolved
   on the other is acceptable and interesting).
5. **A setup→payoff chain** — at least two planted setups (Chekhov details: a
   physical clue, a radio detail) that pay off later; at least one must pay off
   in BOTH world-lines and at least one only in one.

## Constraints

- Fresh and original — no borrowed characters, places, or plot.
- Keep it TAUT: this is a small game, not a novel. Depth over volume.
- You decide every id, title, claim, and edge. The shape above is the only
  contract; the authoring contract (`describe-schema`) is the other.
