Wick's three-tellings and the boy's reasons are consistent with what a frightened child witnessed.

Where the cast thinned, if anywhere: shelter, sc-27 — not an emptying so much as a shift in mode: the final scene reports the secondary cast (Orne, Wick, Ysolt) in narrated summary rather than live action, and Wick gets no closing line of his own. Report and confront stay fully dramatized to the last scene. No road empties to two-or-three principals.

Biggest strength: Voice and knowledge discipline — eight-plus characters each speak in a separable register (the pilot's water-reading cadence, the factor's accountancy, the headman's stone-laying, the salt-wife's self-justifying patter, the child's broken three-tellings), and each person knows only what their own path could earn, with the central murder withheld and then differently disclosed on each road.

Biggest weakness: The shelter road's closing scene (sc-27) drops out of dramatized scene into narrated epilogue, accounting for the cast from outside rather than letting them act; it is the one place where the storytelling tells the aftermath rather than peopling it.

One paragraph: This is a coherent, peopled story told in living voices, and the strongest thing in it is the combination of voice distinctness and knowledge realism — a genuine chorus (pilot, factor, headman, salt-wife, mate, passenger, keeper, the two children) where each person is identifiable by rhythm and reach with the tags hidden, and where nobody ever speaks past what their own night and week would let them know; the supercargo's murder is the proof of this, held back until physical evidence forces it and then disclosed differently on each road (named and caught in report, deliberately never learned in shelter, wrung out by the passenger on the bar in confront). The three branches are genuinely distinct and each follows from its choice — report hands the truth to the Crown, shelter buries it under the salt-house floor at lasting moral cost, confront makes the island judge itself on the bar — so the roads diverge rather than blur. The single weakness is the shelter road's final scene (sc-27), which lapses from dramatized action into narrated summary and leaves the boy without a closing line, the one spot where the story accounts for its people from outside rather than letting them live on the page.
