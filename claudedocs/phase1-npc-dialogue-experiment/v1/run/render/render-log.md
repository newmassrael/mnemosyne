# Render log

## Approach

Three files, one per road (report / shelter / confront). The roads share sc-01
through sc-15 verbatim, then diverge into their own tails (16-21 / 22-27 / 28-32),
matching each outline's scene set exactly.

## Voices

I built each significant person a fixed register and held it across every scene and
every road:

- **Halsa** — plain, weighing, observant; the conscience and the reader's eye. Short
  declaratives when she's sure, questions when she isn't.
- **Orne Veck (headman)** — "lays words like stones in a wall," the chapel cadence;
  speaks *for* the Holm, the long view, survival. His confession on the confront road
  drops that cadence into something older and sea-roughened, so the mask coming off is
  audible.
- **Maon Skerry (pilot)** — gnomic, reads water like text, repeats "here / there,"
  bitter about the iron buoys that took his living; will not name a hand.
- **Bryde Crewe (keeper)** — flat, exhausted, technical about the lamp ("a lamp dies
  slow, gutters, smokes"); guards the one thing it will say.
- **Dunna Quick (salt-wife)** — earthy, tumbling, self-justifying; "a plain gift of
  the sea."
- **Eda (curer)** — flat practical sorrow, crosses herself, believes weather to the
  last on two roads and is undone by the truth on the third.
- **Cass Pellow (mate)** — well-placed grief, the survivor's performance; the prose
  flags it as performance ("his voice cracked, well-placed") without anyone in-scene
  knowing, until it falls off "like a wet coat" at his confession.
- **Ysolt Marran (passenger)** — guarded, genteel, mainland diction; thaws into
  something true at each road's end.
- **Pell Garrow (factor's man)** — a counter; commercial register, "to the cask,"
  trusts only what he's counted himself; his arc is the recalibration of what counts
  as a wrong.
- **Wick (boy)** / **Senna (girl)** — the boy's three-tellings fear vs. the girl's
  flat, clever steadiness ("true you can't argue").
- **The officer** — procedural, Crown-careful, weighs before he writes.

## Knowledge discipline

Each person speaks only from their own outline point of view. No one states the two
load-bearing solutions before their reveal scenes: the false-light-by-Orne lands at
sc-18 (report) / sc-23 (shelter, where it is buried, not exposed) / sc-29 (confront);
the murder-by-the-mate lands at sc-19 (report) and sc-30 (confront) and is **never
revealed on the shelter road** — there it stays an honest drowning, as that outline
withholds it with no reveal scene. Before each reveal the characters circle, suspect,
lie, and fear it (the pilot "will not name a hand"; Halsa feels "the floor shift")
without any line plainly telling it.

## Peopling the tails

Every secondary person given a tail beat speaks it in their own voice: on the report
road Bryde, Ysolt, Pell, Dunna, Eda, the pilot all close out sc-20/21; shelter keeps
Eda, Bryde, Ysolt, Dunna, Wick, Senna present through sc-25/27; confront brings the
whole island onto the bar and keeps Dunna, Maon, Bryde, Pell, Eda, Wick, and Senna
each speaking through sc-31/32. No road empties to two principals in a room.

## What was hard

The shared opening (sc-01-15) had to read identically across all three files while
seeding the two mysteries strongly enough that all three later reveals would land —
without any of the three accidentally over-disclosing. The hardest single line was
keeping the mate's wreck-night account (sc-06) genuinely sympathetic on first read so
the sc-19/sc-30 reveals would turn, while planting a single hint ("well-placed") that
only re-reads as a tell. The shelter road's discipline was the inverse: ending warm
and peopled while the murder stays permanently unspoken and the cost is borne in
silence, not in exposition.
