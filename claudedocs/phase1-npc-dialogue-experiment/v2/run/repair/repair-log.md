# Repair log

## report / sc-16
Replaced the officer's tidy stated-thesis speech ("that's a hanging matter... it's the Holm's neck") with a scene built on pressure and evasion. Halsa now lays the evidence down but cannot bring herself to speak the whole accusation, leaving the things to "lie there between them"; the officer probes by offering her the easy lie ("a lamp that failed... is that what you fetched it to me to write?") and gives her room to be the one who says it. The stakes — that this is no short cask and no fine — surface in the words neither will say (the *rope* she names, the *name* she withholds, the size of it "wanting no saying"), so the reader feels the weight without a character announcing it. Same events: she brings lantern + tally, he opens the inquiry. Same who-knows-what: she still won't name a hand; the murder is still unknown to both.

## confront / sc-29
Re-pitched Orne's reckoning from public oration to a man breaking in his own cracked cadence. His chapel "wall-builder" voice fails him on sight of the lantern ("his mouth opened on the chapel voice and the chapel voice was not there"); the confession is delivered not to the square but person-to-person, mostly to Eda, who has known him forty years — "We were *starving*" spoken across the gap as if explaining over a hearth, broken by her saying only his name. He still owns every fact (carried it, lit it on the Ness, doused the true light, did it for the starving island, never claims it was right). The crowd is present; the talk is intimate and messy under pressure, not address-to-assembly. Wick's owning of the find and Senna at his shoulder are kept.

## confront / sc-30
Turned Ysolt's reveal and Cass's admission from declamation into cut-and-thrust. Ysolt does not orate the deduction; she opens the chest and drives at the mate with hard direct questions ("Where is the key... The sea doesn't unknot a cord from a dead man's neck"), Cass parries thinly, and Halsa lands the conclusion as a slow, surfacing realization ("You held him under") rather than a verdict. The deduction is preserved exactly as earned — the deed, the contested claim, the kin, the key, the bill taken off the body, why he alone lived. Cass's confession is wrung out ugly and sideways ("Is that what you wanted off me"), reluctant rather than composed. Pell's appalled coda is kept intact.

## shelter / sc-27
Converted the closing from outside-summary ("Orne ruled it as before," "Wick grew quiet," "Ysolt did not leave") into a dramatized fireside gathering with the secondary cast on-page in their own voices: Orne silent in his unchanged place, Dunna's frightened over-talk running down, Eda's easy weather-grief (the only voice without a "second floor"), Ysolt's kept-bargain silence, Bryde's still-faced hidden tally, and Senna's "it wears" comfort. Wick gets his own closing line — that he is half-glad he may someday forget which telling was true. Critically, the SHELTER constraint holds: no line states or implies the drowning-that-was-a-killing, which is never learned on this road; the unspoken weight carried is the made-wreck secret (the lantern, the false light) and below it the silence itself. The ending is lived as a held breath, not reported.
