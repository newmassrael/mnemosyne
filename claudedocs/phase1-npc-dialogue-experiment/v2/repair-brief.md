## The four scenes and what each needs

Re-render each scene's PROSE (keep its `## sc-NN` heading and its place in the road).
Keep every person's voice exactly as the surrounding scenes establish it (the pilot
Maon's water-reading cadence, the headman Orne's careful stone-laying speech, the factor
Pell's counting-house diction, the salt-wife Dunna's tumbling self-exculpation, the
keeper Bryde's flat exhaustion, the boy Wick, the girl Senna, the officer, the mate Cass,
the passenger Ysolt, the curer Eda). Keep the SAME EVENTS and the SAME WHO-KNOWS-WHAT —
you are improving how the scene is TOLD, not changing what happens or who knows what.

1. **report / sc-16** — currently the officer lays out the stakes in a tidy speech that
   states things for the reader ("that's a hanging matter... it's the Holm's neck"). Re-
   render so the weight surfaces through PRESSURE and EVASION — what Halsa and the officer
   will not quite say, what each is testing in the other — rather than a stated thesis.
   Let the reader feel the stakes without a character announcing them.

2. **confront / sc-29** — currently the reckoning is pitched as public oration /
   declamation to the gathered crowd. Re-render so people BREAK and OWN it messily and in
   their own registers — Orne's confession in his own broken cadence ("we were starving"),
   not a speech to a square. The crowd can be present; the talk should be person-to-person
   under pressure, not address-to-assembly.

3. **confront / sc-30** — same: the murder reveal currently declaims. Re-render so Ysolt
   forces it and Cass's admission is wrung out, ugly and reluctant — overheard human talk
   breaking open, not a set-piece announcement. Keep the deduction exactly as earned (the
   deed / the kin / the key) but let it surface in the cut and thrust, not a monologue.

4. **shelter / sc-27** — currently the closing scene SUMMARIZES the cast from outside
   (Orne "ruled it as before", Wick "grew quiet", Ysolt "did not leave") and the boy Wick
   gets no line of his own. Re-render it as a DRAMATIZED scene: bring the secondary cast
   on-page in their own voices for a real closing beat — give Wick a closing line, let
   Dunna, Eda, Bryde, Senna, Ysolt each be present and speaking/acting as the silence
   settles over the Holm. Make the ending LIVED, not reported.
   CRITICAL: this is the SHELTER road, where the truth of the wreck-night (the false
   light, and above all the drowning-that-was-a-killing) is NEVER learned by Halsa and
   stays buried. No line in this scene may state or knowingly imply the murder — the
   weight is in what is carried unspoken. Dramatize the SILENCE, not the secret.
