# The Belvoir Letter — a 3-scene slice (prose, frame = ground truth)

## s1 — The sickroom

Since the fever took her in the spring, Helene had not once left the sickroom.
The door was bolted from without each night, and Dr. Marsh kept the only key.
She lay against the bolster, watching the rain, and waited.

## s2 — The conservatory

It was Pell, the under-gardener, who told it first: he had seen Helene at the
conservatory that very evening, pale at the glass, looking down across the wet
lawns toward the lake. He swore to it, and Pell did not lie.

## s3 — The grate

By morning the incriminating letter lay burned to a grey curl of ash in the
study grate. It was never explained. Only Helene had reason to want it gone —
the letter named her, and the letter was now nothing at all.
