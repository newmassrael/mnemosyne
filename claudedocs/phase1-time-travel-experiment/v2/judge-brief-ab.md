# Judge brief (v2, A/B) — two tellings of the same time-travel game

You will read TWO prose tellings of the same short time-travel game — **arm-1** and
**arm-2** — plus a shared player-facing CROSS-AGE BRIEFING. Both are set in one place
(Brae Hollow) that exists in a Founding Age and a Withering Age three generations
later; a traveller with a wend-pipe crosses between them, and early acts changed the
late age. The two tellings present the SAME events in a DIFFERENT order. Read all
three files, then judge.

**Read ONLY this file, the cross-age briefing, and the two prose files you are
given. Do not open any other file.** You are reading cold; judge what is on the
page. Do not assume how either was made, or which came "first."

## Score EACH arm 1–5 (cite scenes)

1. **Reveal impact** — when you learn WHY the late age survives (the cistern, the
   ford), does it land as an earned discovery / a genuine surprise — or is it
   something you were already told earlier, so the "reveal" only restates it?
2. **Era texture** — is the Founding Age vivid as a living place (its people, its
   work, its present), or thin?
3. **Time-travel coherence** — do the two ages lock together; does crossing time
   feel meaningful and clear?
4. **Overall time-travel game** — taken whole, how good a time-travel game is this
   telling?

Give arm-1's four scores, then arm-2's four scores, each with a cited scene.

## The forced A/B call

- **Which arm is the better TIME-TRAVEL game — arm-1 or arm-2 — and WHY?** Name, in
  your own words, what makes the winner work (e.g. the past arriving as a discovery,
  vs. the past lived in full while the late-age people don't know it).
- **What does the LOSING arm do better?** (Be specific — each telling likely has a
  real strength the other lacks.)

## Deliverable

Leave `judge-{your-number}.md` in `run/judges-v2/` with both arms' four scores
(cited), the A/B winner + why, and what the loser does better. Judge independently;
you have not seen the other readers, and you do not know how the tellings were made.
