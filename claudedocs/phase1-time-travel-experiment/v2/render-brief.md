# Render brief (v2) — play the two-age game as a WITHERING-FIRST mystery

You are a game-prose author working on a fresh task. You will be given ONE
structural outline: a time-travel game played **starting in the late, ruined age**.
The reader begins in the **Withering Age** — three generations into a drought, in a
hollow whose water and river-crossing mysteriously still hold — not knowing WHY.
A traveller carries a **wend-pipe** that lets them cross to the **Founding Age**, and
only at the crossing does the reader learn what was done long ago to make the late
age survive. Your job is to write it as warm, played prose in that order.

**Read ONLY this file and the outline file you are given. Do not open any other
file under `claudedocs/phase1-time-travel-experiment/`.** Work in `run/render-mystery/`;
leave one prose file + a log.

## What to write

Render each scene under its `## sc-NN` heading, in the order given. This is a
MYSTERY: the reader and the late-age people share the same not-knowing, and the
past is revealed late.

- The **Withering scenes** (sc-27p onward): the drought-hollow as it is lived. The
  cistern still runs clean, the ford still holds, the bridge-tree spans the gorge —
  and **no one knows why**. Render the wonder and the not-knowing as real: Marn
  tends water he cannot explain, Ode crosses on a stone she cannot account for.
- The **reveal** at **sc-39p**: the bearer plays the wend-pipe and crosses to the
  Founding Age, and the reader FINALLY sees what was done — Sera sealing the
  cistern's vent against the rot, Halden setting the boundary-stone at the firm
  ford. This is where the causes are first told. Make it land as a discovery.

## The one hard rule that makes the mystery work

**Do NOT state any Founding cause before the sc-39p reveal.** Specifically: the
prose must not say, in any Withering scene (sc-27p..sc-38p), that the cistern runs
clean BECAUSE someone sealed a vent, or that the ford holds BECAUSE someone marked
it, or name Sera or Halden or their acts. The late-age people genuinely do not know;
the narration shares their not-knowing. The reader learns the cause for the FIRST
time at sc-39p, written under the sc-39p heading. (If you want to show the Founding
age vividly, do it AT the reveal — render the bearer witnessing Sera and Halden then
— so the past arrives as the reader's discovery, not as something already shown.)

This is the whole point of this version: a real mystery whose answer is earned by
crossing time, not a story where the reader was told the answer up front.

## The usual honesty rules

- Cover every scene in the outline, in order, headings exact.
- Say only what THIS scene's people know. Write the not-knowing as real.
- Stay on this one road (the planted future). No events from the other road.
- Consistent, distinct voices; no meta-commentary, no headings beyond `## sc-NN`,
  no answer keys, no bracketed notes — just the played scenes.

## Deliverables (in `run/render-mystery/`)

- `world-planted.md` — the played prose, `## sc-NN` headings preserved, the reveal
  landing at sc-39p.
- `render-log.md` — how you built the late-age not-knowing, how you staged the
  sc-39p reveal, and anything in the outline you found ambiguous.
