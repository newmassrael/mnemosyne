# Render log — Withering-first mystery (world-planted.md)

## The governing constraint

This telling starts in the ruined late age and withholds every Founding
cause until sc-39p. The late-age people and the reader share one
not-knowing. So the whole build was organised around a single discipline:
the Withering scenes (sc-27p..sc-38p) may render the *effects* — clean
water, a firm ford, a living bridge — with full force, but may not name,
imply, or supply their *causes*. No sealed vent, no marked ford, no rot,
no Sera, no Halden, until the bearer crosses time and witnesses them.

## How I built the late-age not-knowing

I made the not-knowing an active emotional texture, not just an omission:

- **sc-27p / sc-29p** establish the rule of the world: every other spring
  in the valley failed, and the survivors cannot say why this one didn't.
  Marn even goes on a "pilgrimage of dread" to the dead springs looking
  for the difference and finds none — so the mystery is foregrounded as a
  thing the characters have actively investigated and failed to solve. The
  drought is total; the survival is the anomaly.
- **sc-28p** lets Marn explain what he *can* explain (the water is good
  because he tends it — visible, true) and then explicitly opens the gap:
  what keeps the *source* good "under all his keeping" is the question he
  has never answered. This separates earned knowledge from the withheld
  cause without leaking the cause.
- **sc-32p / sc-33p** (Pell) carry the not-knowing for the craft-row /
  boundary thread. Pell knows of no dispute, no names, no stone. I framed
  the dead row as "the country of the forgotten," so the *absence* of the
  Founding dispute is itself rendered — the reader feels a silence that
  was once a noise, without being told what the noise was.
- **sc-34p / sc-36p / sc-37p / sc-38p** give the ford, the crossing, and
  the bridge-tree the same treatment: things the folk "trust the way you
  trust the ground — completely, and without a story." Each effect is
  shown holding; each origin is explicitly marked as unknown and unasked.

The two "the bearer recognises / finds a mark" beats (sc-30p, sc-31p) and
the "Ode reads the stone" beat (sc-35p) were the riskiest, because the
outline's scene labels name Sera/Halden and one bullet even states the
cause ("because Sera sealed the vent"). I treated those as outline
metadata and the per-scene MYSTERY DISCIPLINE notes as authoritative.

- **sc-30p**: the bearer presses the lower seam and says "you know this,"
  but Marn genuinely *doesn't* — he has never looked below the waterline.
  The bearer's recognition is rendered as visible behaviour (a familiar,
  returned-to gesture) that Marn observes from the outside and cannot
  interpret. The reader sees that the stranger knows something; the reader
  is not told what.
- **sc-31p**: the keeper's mark is shown as a real, traceable object — but
  unreadable, nameless, of unknown hand and purpose. Crucially I placed
  the mark and the unfailing water side by side and had Marn explicitly
  refuse the causal link: "Whether the one had anything to do with the
  other, Marn could not say. He had no way to know." The cause is dangled
  as a possible connection the character cannot confirm, never as fact.
- **sc-35p**: Ode reads the *boundary-stone's function* (a stone set on
  purpose to mark a firm place) — which is plain to a warden — but cannot
  read its lines and cannot know who set it, when, or how they knew. The
  stone says "here," not "why."

## How I staged the sc-39p reveal

The reveal is the only place any Founding cause is stated, and I wanted it
to land as the bearer's (and reader's) discovery, not as exposition.

- **The crossing is sensory, not explained.** The wend-pipe is played; the
  dry valley visibly "thins" backward — dust drawing in, the trickle
  swelling, the dead row crowding with carts — so the time-cross is
  experienced before any fact arrives.
- **Causes arrive as witnessed action, in present unfolding.** Sera is
  watched *finding* the rot, *recognising* it, *sealing* it, then *cutting*
  the mark — in that order, as events the bearer sees happen, not as a
  summary of what once happened. Same for Halden: watched reading the
  river, judging the future channel, cutting and setting the stone.
- **Payoff loops back to the planted effects.** Each Founding act is
  pinned to the late-age wonder it explains, and I reused exact earlier
  images so recognition snaps shut: Sera "pressed it once to test it, the
  very gesture the bearer's own hand had unknowingly repeated" (callback
  to sc-30p); "the same mark... that Marn had traced and could not read"
  (sc-31p); "the same stone... that Ode had turned up out of the silt"
  (sc-35p). The dye-cart dispute pays off Pell's forgotten quarrel (sc-33p).
- **Emotional figure of the reveal: reach across time.** I leaned on the
  fact that neither Founder acted for themselves — Sera seals "for whoever
  should keep it after her," Halden marks for "the ages after him," the
  stone "had been looking, across all those years, straight at" Ode. So
  the discovery isn't just mechanism; it's that two strangers held the
  dying hollow up from three generations back. The closing paragraph names
  the answer the whole telling withheld: "Not luck. Not mercy owed."
- **sc-40p** returns to the dry age and restates the holding effects
  (water, ford, bridge) now that the reader carries the cause — so the
  ending lands as earned reassurance rather than fresh mystery.

## Ambiguities I resolved

- **Outline labels and one bullet leak the cause.** The italic scene
  descriptors name Sera (sc-30p) and Halden (sc-35p), and sc-31p's first
  bullet is a `(TRUTH)` reading "...because Sera sealed the vent." The
  per-scene `[MYSTERY DISCIPLINE]` notes and the brief's one hard rule
  directly forbid surfacing these before sc-39p. I treated the descriptors
  and that TRUTH bullet as structural metadata (the omniscient ground
  truth the mystery conceals), rendered the *characters'* knowledge only,
  and surfaced every named cause for the first time at sc-39p. Verified:
  "Sera" and "Halden" occur only within sc-39p; "rot" occurs nowhere
  before it; the few pre-reveal uses of "because" are all in-character,
  non-Founding (e.g. "the water is good because Marn keeps it").
- **The bearer's identity / continuity.** The outline implies one
  recurring bearer (the wend-pipe carrier of sc-30p who returns at sc-39p,
  and per sc-37p's metadata the planter of the gorge-sapling). I rendered
  the bearer as a single continuous figure across sc-30p, sc-31p, and
  sc-39p, but did NOT have them voice or explain anything in the Withering
  scenes — their knowledge stays internal/behavioural there so it can't
  leak the cause. The sapling-planting is left implicit (sc-37p's TRUTH is
  "has grown to a living bridge-tree"); I rendered the bridge as a present
  wonder and did not assert who planted it before the reveal, to keep the
  mystery clean.
- **"begins/ends/holding" counters.** Treated as outline bookkeeping, not
  prose content; not rendered.
