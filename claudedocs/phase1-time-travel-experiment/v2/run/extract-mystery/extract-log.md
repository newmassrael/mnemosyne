## Scope of the prose given

The prose runs `## sc-27p` through `## sc-40p` — fourteen scenes, ALL in the
Withering Age on the `planted` road. There are NO shared Founding scenes
(`sc-01`..`sc-26`) in this manuscript; the telling opens already three
generations into the drought and only reaches the Founding Age once, inside
`sc-39p`, when the bearer time-travels back. Per the task framing, every fact
is tagged `branch: planted` (there is no `main`/Founding trunk present to tag).

## THE CRITICAL QUESTION — when does the prose FIRST commit each Founding cause?

The re-read tracks the exact scene at which the prose first states, as ground
truth, the CAUSE behind each surviving wonder.

### (i) The cistern's seal / why it runs clean

FIRST stated as a Founding cause at: **`## sc-39p`** (the final time-travel
scene, second-to-last in the manuscript).

Up to and including `sc-38p`, the clean unfailing water is presented ONLY as an
unexplained wonder, and the prose is emphatic that no cause is known:
- `sc-27p`: "No one in the hollow can tell you why this one and no other."
- `sc-28p`: "What keeps the source good ... is another question, and it is not
  one Marn has ever been able to answer."
- `sc-29p`: "Marn does not understand it. He has no story for it that
  satisfies him."
- `sc-30p`–`sc-31p`: the traveller finds the low seam and the worn keeper's
  mark, but the prose explicitly states Marn cannot read the mark and CANNOT
  say whether "the one had anything to do with the other" (mark vs. water). At
  this point neither Marn NOR the traveller is given a cause — "I could not
  have said what the stranger saw there." So I recorded at sc-30p/sc-31p only
  the OBSERVED facts (a seam exists; a worn keeper's mark exists; the water
  rises clean) and the explicit NOT-knowing — NOT a Founding cause.

It is only at `sc-39p` that the narration commits: Sera found a creeping rot at
the cistern's lower vent, packed/sealed the vent, and cut the keeper's mark for
a later keeper. That is where the cistern's seal / why-clean first becomes
ground truth in this prose. Recorded as facts `f-39p-02` (vent rotting),
`f-39p-03` (vent sealed), `f-39p-04` (Sera cut the mark).

### (ii) The ford's marking / why it holds

FIRST stated as a Founding cause at: **`## sc-39p`** (same final time-travel
scene).

Up to and including `sc-38p`, the firm crossing is an unexplained wonder:
- `sc-34p`: Ode "keeps the crossing" but it is just "the river's moods."
- `sc-35p`: Ode digs up a boundary-stone and reads that "someone, in some age
  past all the hollow's remembering, had set this stone exactly where the
  crossing held firm" — but "could not say who, or when, or how they had
  known. The stone said nothing of that." So sc-35p commits only that a
  purpose-set stone EXISTS and sits on the firm line — NOT who set it or why
  the line holds. Recorded that way (`f-35p-01`, `f-35p-02`).
- `sc-36p`: "None of them knew its origin. ... they trusted it the way you
  trust the ground ... and called it luck." Explicitly NO cause.

The cause first becomes ground truth at `sc-39p`: Halden the dyer read the
river to judge which ford would stay firm across the coming ages, and cut +
set the boundary-stone at the one firm ford (settling the craft-row dispute in
the same stone). Recorded as `f-39p-06` (the one firm line) and `f-39p-07`
(Halden cut/set the boundary-stone = the same stone Ode found).

### Verdict on the critical question

BOTH Founding causes — (i) the cistern seal/why-clean and (ii) the ford
marking/why-holds — are FIRST stated only at the late time-travel scene
**`## sc-39p`**. No earlier scene names a Founding cause; the earlier
late-age scenes (sc-27p..sc-38p) describe the clean water and the firm
crossing strictly as unexplained wonders, and I recorded them as such (the
wonder + the explicit not-knowing), NOT as a cause. The final scene `sc-40p`
restates that the three things hold but, again, names no NEW cause (it points
back to "Marn's keeping," "Ode's folk," "Tamsin to cross"); the causal
attribution lives wholly in `sc-39p`.

## A third wonder with NO Founding cause anywhere in the prose

The bridge-tree at the dry gorge (`gorge-sapling`) is introduced `sc-37p` and
used `sc-38p`/`sc-40p` as a living span that "should not be," but the prose
NEVER gives it a Founding cause — not even at `sc-39p`. The sc-39p time-travel
reveals only TWO Founding deeds (Sera's seal, Halden's ford); the tree is left
"gives no sign of why a tree should have chosen to grow exactly there." I
therefore recorded the bridge-tree only as an observed wonder (`f-37p-01`,
`f-37p-02`, `f-38p-01`) with no cause fact. This is a deliberate gap in the
prose, not an omission in the extraction.

## Ambiguities / judgment calls

1. Branch tag: vocab.md says Founding scenes → `main`, Withering `p`-scenes →
   `planted`. But this manuscript has NO `sc-01..sc-26` Founding scenes at all;
   even the Founding-Age content (Sera/Halden) is narrated INSIDE the Withering
   scene `sc-39p`. Per the task's explicit instruction ("there is no
   `main`/Founding trunk in this telling ... tag facts branch `planted`"), I
   tagged every fact `planted`. Flagging the divergence from vocab.md's generic
   note for the comparison.
2. `cistern` vs `cistern-vent`: the prose distinguishes the cistern (the water
   body, `condition` clean / still-giving) from the lower vent/seam where the
   rot was and the seal went (`cistern-vent`, `condition` rotting → sealed). I
   typed the water-state facts on `cistern` and the rot/seal facts on
   `cistern-vent`, matching the prose's own spatial distinction (the seam
   "below the wet line" vs. the brimming cistern above).
3. `sc-39p` payoff/causation is stated by the NARRATION (the bearer "saw" it,
   "the bearer understood"), not merely spoken by a character. I recorded the
   deed-facts (rot found, vent sealed, mark cut, river read, stone set) under
   frame `gt` because the narration presents them as simply true in the
   Founding Age the bearer witnesses, and the interpretive "held the hollow up"
   synthesis under frame `bearer` (`f-39p-05`, `f-39p-08`).
4. Predicate `object_kind`: vocab.md's `condition` example shows literal state
   values, so I declared `condition` with `object_kind: scalar` and used
   `{kind:"value",...}` objects (e.g. cistern clean, vent sealed/rotting, ford
   firm, tree spanning). `possession` takes an entity object, so
   `object_kind: entity`.
