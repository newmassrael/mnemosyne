# Render brief (Stage 2) — play a slice of the two-age game as warm prose

You are a game-prose author. You will be given STRUCTURAL OUTLINES of a
time-travel game — a Founding-Age trunk and ONE Withering-Age future through to
its ending — each a sequence of scenes with, per scene, what is true, who is
present, what they know, and which threads are in play. The traveller carries a
**wend-pipe** that moves them between the early age and the late age standing on
the same ground. Your job is to write it out as warm, played PROSE: the
scene-by-scene narration a good game would give a player living across the two
ages.

**Read ONLY this file and the outline files you are given. Do not open any other
file under `claudedocs/phase1-time-travel-experiment/`.** Work in `run/render/`;
leave one prose file per outline.

## What to write

For each scene (each `## sc-NN` heading in the outline, in order), write the scene
as it is PLAYED: the place (and which age it is — the Founding Age young and busy,
the Withering Age the same ground three generations older and under drought), what
the traveller sees and does, the people they meet and what is said, the turn the
scene takes. Warm and grounded — a told story, not a bulleted summary. When the
traveller crosses ages with the wend-pipe, let the cross be felt: the same place,
older or younger, the same prop worn or new. Let an early act and its late
consequence connect as the player would feel it — recognising in the late age the
mark left in the early one.

## The rules that keep it honest

- **Cover every scene in the outline, in order, under its `## sc-NN` heading.**
  Keep the headings exactly as given so the scenes stay aligned.
- **Say only what THIS scene and its people know.** The outline tells you, per
  scene, what each person knows and what is still hidden. A Founding-Age CAUSE that
  the late age has not yet learned must NOT be voiced in a Withering-Age scene
  before the traveller crosses to witness it — a cause revealed at the crossing
  scene must not be stated earlier on this road, and a late-age character must not
  explain a cause they had no way to know. Write the not-knowing as real: the
  late-age people act on what they live with, not on the early-age cause they never
  saw.
- **Stay on this road.** You are writing ONE future. Do not pull in events that
  happen only on the other future of the planting fork.
- Write in a consistent register throughout. Distinct, consistent voices for the
  early-age and late-age people. No meta-commentary, no headings beyond the
  `## sc-NN` scene markers, no answer keys, no bracketed notes — just the played
  scenes.

## Deliverables (in `run/render/`)

- One `world-<road>.md` per outline (e.g. `world-trunk.md` + `world-planted.md`,
  or whatever the outlines are named) — the played prose, `## sc-NN` headings
  preserved.
- `render-log.md` — a short note on the voice choices you made (how you
  distinguished the two ages) and anything in the outline you found ambiguous.
