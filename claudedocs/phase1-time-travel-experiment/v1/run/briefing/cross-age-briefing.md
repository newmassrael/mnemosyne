# Brae Hollow — a cross-age briefing

One place, two ages. A traveller with a wend-pipe moves between them, standing on
the same ground. What people do in the **Founding Age** changes the **Withering
Age** three generations later. (Rendered slice for this reading: the Founding
trunk, then the *planted* future.)

## The three places, in both ages

| Place | Founding Age | Withering Age (drought, 3 generations on) |
|---|---|---|
| **Cistern Steps** (the water) | Sera keeps the spring; she finds a creeping rot in the vent | Marn keeps the cistern — the last good water in the valley |
| **Loom Quarter** (the work) | A busy craft-row; Halden the boundary-mason; a dispute over the ford | The craft-row gone quiet; Pell works the last loom |
| **Wend Fields** (the living) | Green farmland, a busy river crossing | Ode keeps the crossing; the channel has shifted |

## The causal chains — what the early age did to the late age

1. **The cistern (same place, across the ages).** Founding: Sera packs and seals
   the cistern's lower vent against the rot (sc-04) and cuts a keeper's mark beside
   it (sc-05). Withering: the cistern still runs clean — the last good water —
   *because* she sealed it (sc-31p); the bearer finds her mark still cut in the
   stone.

2. **The ford (one corner reaching another, generations later).** Founding: Halden,
   in the **Loom Quarter**, cuts and sets a boundary-stone to mark the only ford
   that stays firm when the channel shifts — for a craft-row dispute, never
   imagining a drought (sc-09–sc-12). Withering: Ode, in the **Wend Fields**, finds
   the half-buried boundary-stone and reads it (sc-35p), and the Withering folk
   cross to the last green field on that firm ford — *because a dyer marked it in an
   age none of them remember* (sc-36p). A different corner, a different person, an
   act and its consequence three generations apart.

3. **The planting (a choice that splits the late age).** Founding: at the Wend
   crossing the bearer may plant a gorge-sapling — or not (sc-26, the one fork).
   - *Planted future (this slice):* the sapling has grown to a living bridge-tree
     spanning the dry gorge (sc-37p); Tamsin reaches and works the far field across
     it (sc-38p); the far field is reachable.
   - *Barren future (the other road):* nothing grew, the gorge stays impassable,
     and the far field is lost.

## What the late age cannot see (the reveal)

In the Withering Age nobody knows why the cistern still runs or who marked the
ford — those Founding causes are lost to the late age. Only the bearer, who crosses
between the ages, can connect them: at sc-39p the bearer crosses back to the
Founding Age and witnesses why the cistern and the ford hold — Sera's seal and
Halden's stone. The hollow withers, but the far field carries it: water, ford, and
bridge hold (sc-40p).
