# Judge 2 — verdict

## Scores (1–5)

1. **Era consistency — 5.**
   The cistern's lower vent is the spine of this. In the Founding age Sera works
   "the lower vent, where the overflow runs back into the rock" (sc-04); in the
   Withering age the bearer stops at "the basin's lower vent, where the overflow
   runs back" and recognises "the same vent. The same stone, the same seam, older
   now and dust-greyed" (sc-30p). The same geometry, named the same way, aged. The
   Loom Quarter likewise reads as one lane across time: a busy craft-row "all
   clatter — looms knocking, a dyer's paddle" (sc-01/sc-07) becomes "dust and shut
   doors and the wind moving through empty frames," with "one loom" left (sc-32p).
   The late age is plainly the same ground worn down, not a new place reusing the
   name.

2. **Causal-payoff satisfaction — 5.**
   The seal pays off cleanly. Sera packs the rot "course by course... sealing the
   rot off from the running water" (sc-04); three generations on "the cistern...
   runs clean... with every other spring soured or dead" (sc-31p), and the mark
   she cut — "three strokes and a notch" (sc-05) — is found again, "a small cut,
   three strokes and a notch, worn but plain" (sc-31p). The cause is shown in full
   on the page before its consequence is reached, and the keeper's mark is the
   physical placement that makes it a real consequence rather than coincidence.
   Marn's not knowing why (sc-29p) sharpens rather than weakens it.

3. **Cross-region coherence — 5.**
   The ford is the one-corner-reaching-another thread, and it lines up exactly.
   Halden, in the Loom Quarter, reads the river and cuts a boundary-stone for "a
   craft-row squabble over cart-tracks" marking "the one ford that stays" (sc-09–
   sc-12), explicitly "never dreaming of drought" (sc-39p). Generations later, in
   a different corner — the Wend Fields — Ode's boot turns up "an edge of cut stone
   half-buried in the silt," reads "a mark of the firm ford" (sc-35p), and the folk
   cross "because a stone in an old hand told Ode where the firm ford lay" (sc-36p).
   Loom-Quarter act, Wend-Fields payoff, "reasons washed out" — it reads as one
   causal thread travelling across the hollow and the years, exactly the briefing's
   chain 2.

4. **Time-travel legibility — 5.**
   The pipe mechanic is taught explicitly and early ("stand on this ground young,
   play, and stand on this same ground old," sc-17), so I always know which age I
   am in: the late-age scenes are tagged "p" and open on the drought (sc-27p). The
   reveal is held back correctly — in sc-31p the bearer "say[s] nothing to Marn,"
   the why deliberately withheld — and only lands at sc-39p when the bearer crosses
   back and *witnesses* "why the cistern runs clean... why the ford holds." The
   reveal arrives at the moment of witnessing, not before.

5. **Overall time-travel game — 5.**
   Three causal chains (seal, ford, planting), one of them a real fork (sc-26
   plant-or-not; this slice is the planted road, sc-37p–sc-38p), a taught traversal
   mechanic, and a withheld-then-witnessed reveal. It reads as a coherent game
   across time, not an outline.

## Two forced calls

- **What most makes it feel like time travel:** the lower vent of the cistern —
  watched being packed and marked on a bright morning (sc-04/sc-05) and then
  recognised, same seam and same three-strokes-and-a-notch mark, "older now and
  dust-greyed," in the drought (sc-30p/sc-31p). One spot, two ages, the bearer's
  memory bridging them.

- **What most undercuts it:** the planting fork is the thinnest of the three.
  Its Founding cause is only a *choice* the bearer weighs (sc-24–sc-26), not an
  act witnessed completed the way the seal and the stone are; the bridge-tree
  simply exists at sc-37p. It pays off, but with less earned weight than the two
  threads whose causes we watch land in full.

## Verdict

**A real time-travel game.** Two ages of one ground lock together through shared,
aged-in landmarks; early acts are shown, then their late consequences reached and
the cause witnessed at the right moment — one place before and after, not two eras
stapled together.
