# Judge 1 — verdict

## Scores

**1. Era consistency — 5**
The two ages lock onto the same ground at the level of specific stone. The
lower vent Sera packs course-on-course (sc-04) is recognised by the bearer as
"the same vent. The same stone, the same seam, older now and dust-greyed"
(sc-30p). The loud craft-row of dye-vats and shuttles (sc-07) is the same lane
now reduced to "dust and shut doors and the wind moving through empty frames"
with one loom knocking (sc-32p). The river crossing busy with carts (sc-01) is
the thin brown line Ode reads season on season (sc-34p). The Withering hollow
is unmistakably the Founding hollow grown old and dry, not a different place
sharing a name.

**2. Causal-payoff satisfaction — 5**
Both early acts land as placed, earned late consequences. Sera seals the rot
"for whoever keeps the Steps after I'm gone" (sc-04) and the payoff is explicit
and felt: "three generations into the withering, with every other spring soured
or dead, the water that comes off these Steps comes cold and clear" — and the
bearer finds her three-strokes-and-a-notch mark still cut in the grey stone
(sc-31p). The bone-under-gravel firm ford Halden reads and beds in stone (sc-09,
sc-11) is exactly the half-buried boundary-stone Ode digs up and reads true
against the channel (sc-35p), and the folk cross dry-shod on it (sc-36p). The
causes are concrete, not coincidental.

**3. Cross-region coherence — 5**
The ford chain is the standout: an act in one corner reaches a person in a
different corner generations later, and the places and times line up as one
thread. Halden cuts the stone in the **Loom Quarter** for a craft-row cart
dispute, "never dreaming of drought" (sc-10, sc-12); generations on, Ode in the
**Wend Fields** turns the stone up in the silt and reads it true (sc-35p), and
the Withering folk cross on it (sc-36p) — "set there by someone in an age none
of them remember, for reasons washed out long ago." The distant corner, distant
generation, and original act read as a single consequence travelling across the
hollow and the years, exactly as the briefing's chain 2 frames it.

**4. Time-travel legibility — 5**
It is always clear which age you stand in, and the reveal is held until it is
earned. Founding scenes show the acts as ordinary labour, plainly flagged that
no one knows their weight (sc-05, sc-12); the bearer learns the pipe moves
between ages on the same ground (sc-17) and grasps the cause-effect shape
(sc-22). Crucially, the *why* is withheld through the Withering age — Marn
cannot name why his cistern alone runs (sc-29p), Ode can only guess "somebody
knew this water" (sc-35p) — and lands precisely when the bearer crosses back to
witness the seal and the stone being made (sc-39p). The reveal fires at the
witnessing, not before.

**5. Overall time-travel game — 5**
Taken whole this reads as a coherent time-travel game: one place in two ages, a
clear mechanic (play the pipe, same ground, two times), three causal chains
(cistern, ford, planting), and one real fork — plant the gorge-sapling or not
(sc-26) — whose planted road pays off in the bridge-tree Tamsin works (sc-37p,
sc-38p) while the barren road is named as the lost alternative (briefing chain
3). sc-40p closes the loop cleanly: "What was done on a bright forgotten morning
holds the dry age up."

## Two forced calls

- **What most makes it feel like time travel:** Halden's boundary-stone — cut in
  the craft-row for a petty cart dispute (sc-10) and dug out of the silt by Ode
  a different corner and three generations later (sc-35p) — because the bearer
  alone, having watched both, holds the single causal thread that the late age
  has wholly lost.

- **What most undercuts it:** the barren-future alternative is only described in
  the briefing, not shown on the page; with only the planted road rendered, the
  one genuine fork (sc-26) lands more as a foregone payoff than as a live choice
  whose other outcome we have witnessed.

**Verdict:** A real time-travel game — two ages of one hollow that lock together
at the level of specific stone, with early acts withheld as mysteries in the
late age and revealed exactly when the bearer crosses back to witness their
making.
