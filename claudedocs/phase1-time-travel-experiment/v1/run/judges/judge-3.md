# Judge 3 — verdict

## Scores

1. **Era consistency — 5.** The Cistern Steps are unmistakably the same ground in
   both ages: sc-03 finds the rot "in the vent" of the lower overflow; sc-30p
   returns to "the basin's lower vent, under the grey light" and names it — "the
   same vent. The same stone, the same seam, older now and dust-greyed." The
   Loom Quarter degrades coherently too: the loud craft-row of sc-07 ("dye-vats
   steam, weavers throw their shuttles") becomes sc-32p's "dust and shut doors
   and the wind moving through empty frames," one loom still knocking. The late
   age reads as the early ground worn down, not a different place with the same
   name.

2. **Causal-payoff satisfaction — 5.** The cistern chain lands cleanly: Sera
   packs and seals the vent against the rot (sc-04) and cuts her mark (sc-05);
   three generations on the water "comes cold and clear and good" while every
   other spring has soured (sc-31p), and the bearer finds "a small cut, three
   strokes and a notch" — exactly Sera's mark from sc-05. The cause is placed,
   not coincidental: sc-39p shows the seal as "the seal under all that good
   water." Marn's not knowing why (sc-29p) makes the payoff felt rather than
   merely asserted.

3. **Cross-region coherence — 5.** The ford is the strongest thread: Halden, in
   the Loom Quarter, reads the river and cuts a boundary-stone for a cart-dispute
   (sc-09–sc-11), "never dreaming of drought" (sc-12, sc-39p). Generations later
   and in a different corner — the Wend Fields — Ode's boot turns up "an edge of
   cut stone half-buried in the silt" (sc-35p), reads "a mark of the firm ford,"
   and the folk cross dry-shod on it (sc-36p). A craft-row act reaching a
   field-warden across the hollow and the years reads as one causal thread, with
   the briefing's chain 2 matched on the page.

4. **Time-travel legibility — 5.** The mechanism is established before it is used
   (sc-17: "stand on this ground young, play, and stand on this same ground old")
   and the ages are always clearly tagged (grey/dust/drought vs. loud/green). The
   reveal is held until earned: sc-31p deliberately withholds ("you say nothing
   to Marn... not yours to hand him here"), and the full witnessing of *why*
   lands at sc-39p when the bearer crosses back and watches Sera seal and Halden
   cut — "Now you know the why, and you carry it across." It lands at the moment
   of witnessing, not before.

5. **Overall time-travel game — 5.** Three causal chains (cistern, ford, the one
   planted fork at sc-26), a real player choice with two divergent futures named
   in the briefing, a clean mechanic, and a reveal structured around the
   traveller's privileged crossing. sc-40p closes the loop with felt weight:
   "What was done on a bright forgotten morning holds the dry age up." This reads
   as a coherent game across time, not an outline.

## Two forced calls

- **What most makes it feel like time travel:** the cross-region ford thread —
  Halden cutting the stone for a petty cart-squabble (sc-10–sc-12) and Ode
  digging that same stone out of the dead channel a different corner and three
  generations on (sc-35p–sc-36p), the giver forgotten, the gift still holding.

- **What most undercuts it:** the planting fork is the thinnest leg as rendered —
  the choice is laid out at sc-26 and its planted payoff appears at sc-37p–sc-38p,
  but on this slice we only ever see the planted road; the barren alternative is
  named only in the briefing, so the fork's stakes are told more than the cistern
  and ford causes are shown.

## Verdict

**A real time-travel game.** Two ages of one place lock together stone-by-stone,
two hidden Founding causes pay off as earned Withering consequences across both
time and the hollow, and the reveal is withheld and then witnessed at the right
moment — coherent across time, not two eras stapled together.
