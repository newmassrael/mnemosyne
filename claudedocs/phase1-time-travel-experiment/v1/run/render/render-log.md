# Render log

## Voice — distinguishing the two ages

I held the two ages apart mostly through rhythm, sensory register, and what the
prose is allowed to *know*.

**Founding Age (trunk)** — present-tense feel even in past narration, loud and
busy, short active clauses, sounds of work everywhere (looms knocking, the dye
paddle, the spring "throwing light"). The keepers (Sera, Halden) speak plainly
and a little proudly of ordinary craft; their dialogue treats large acts as
small chores — "Mine to mind," "A stone's still saying the same thing when we're
all dead." Crucially, the Founding voice carries *no weight of the future*: per
the trunk's own instruction I rendered the sealing and the stone-setting as
present events and did not foreshadow their later significance. Sera seals a
vent against rot; Halden settles a cart-dispute in stone; neither imagines a
drought. The bearer watches and "files it away as nothing more."

**Withering Age (planted)** — slower cadence, longer falling sentences, a dust-
and-grey palette, sound replaced by silence (the one knocking loom against the
dead row). The late-age keepers (Marn, Pell, Ode, Tamsin) speak from *loss and
not-knowing*: they tend gifts whose givers are forgotten. Where the Founding
voice is confident, the Withering voice is faithful-but-uncertain — Marn keeps
water he can't explain, Ode trusts a stone in "an old hand," Pell says the
reasons "wash out." The drought is the constant pressure behind every line.

The crossings themselves I tried to make *felt*: same ground, same props, older
or younger — the bearer recognising "the same vent" and "the same Steps" from
the other side of the pipe, the gorge-sapling of sc-20 returning as the
bridge-tree of sc-37p.

## Reveal discipline — how I held the cause back

This was the load-bearing constraint. The planted outline names the Founding
causes in its TRUTH/belief lines (Sera's seal, Halden's stone) as *reference for
me*, but the rules forbid voicing them in any Withering scene before the bearer
crosses back at **sc-39p**. So through sc-27p–sc-38p I rendered only
*consequences as lived*, never causes:

- **sc-31p** (clean water): I rendered the enormous plain fact — the cistern
  still runs clean and cold while every other spring has died — and had the
  bearer find "a keeper's mark... set here by someone the late age does not
  remember," a worn cut in old stone. I deliberately did *not* write "because
  Sera sealed the vent." The bearer half-knows but the text states the bearer
  "says nothing... because the thing you half-know is not yours to hand him
  here." The cause is present as an unread mark and a withheld recognition, not
  as a stated reason. Marn never even notices the mark.

- **sc-36p** (firm ford): I rendered the consequence — the folk cross dry-shod
  on a ford that holds while the river dies — and attributed it only as far as
  Ode's own knowledge goes: "a stone in an old hand told Ode where the firm ford
  lay — set there by someone in an age none of them remember, for reasons washed
  out long ago." No mason, no name, no "because Halden marked it." The faith is
  named ("takes it on faith... a gift with the giver forgotten") but the giver
  is not.

- **sc-39p** is where the dam breaks: only here, with the bearer physically
  crossing back and watching, do I write the causal sentences out loud — "This
  is why the cistern runs clean," "This is why the ford holds," "Sera's seal and
  Halden's stone." The two earlier consequence-scenes were written so that this
  reveal lands as new knowledge, not a repeat.

- **sc-40p** (ending) then uses the now-revealed causal frame ("What was done on
  a bright forgotten morning holds the dry age up"), which is legitimate because
  the bearer has crossed and knows.

## Ambiguities I had to resolve

1. **The bearer's knowledge in the late age.** The bearer genuinely watched the
   Founding acts (trunk sc-15/sc-16), so by sc-30p–sc-31p the bearer *recognises*
   the vent and could in principle name the cause. The outline still reserves the
   full causal statement for sc-39p ("witnesses why"). I read this as: the bearer
   may *recognise the place and the mark* in the late age (which sc-30p/sc-31p
   explicitly grant) but the *narration must not assert the causal "because"* —
   and the bearer must not explain it to the late-age folk — until the deliberate
   return-and-witness at sc-39p. So I let the bearer recognise and stay silent,
   holding the stated cause for the crossing.

2. **sc-26 is a CHOICE, but this is the *planted* road**, where the choice was to
   plant (the bridge-tree exists at sc-37p). I rendered trunk sc-26 as the choice
   still open at the lip (per the trunk outline's wording, "plant... or do not"),
   and did not pre-decide it in the trunk file; the planted future simply shows
   the planted outcome. The two files stay on their own roads — I pulled in no
   events from the unplanted future.

3. **"Holding" thread counts and the begins/ends bracket annotations** I treated
   as structural bookkeeping, not prose content, and did not surface them.
