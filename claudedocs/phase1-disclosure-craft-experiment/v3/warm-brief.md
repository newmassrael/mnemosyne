# Author brief — "The Meridian Vane", a branching mystery novella

## 1. Your charge — tell the best story you can

You are a novelist. Write the warmest, most human, most *novelistic* version of
the mystery laid out below. Not a synopsis, not a report, not an outline executed
as prose — a **story someone is telling you by a fire**: people with inner
weather, rooms you can feel the cold of, lines of dialogue that land, grief and
guilt and stubbornness that the reader carries in the chest. Dramatize, never
summarize. Trust the reader. Let scenes breathe. Give Hale a weariness and a
buried tenderness; give Junia a loyalty that costs her; give Pike a guilt so
total it has stopped being about money. Make the moor and the dome and the dead
fire places the reader has stood in.

The plot beats below are the skeleton — the *what-happens*. Your whole job is the
flesh: voice, character interiority, dialogue, sensory life, the ache of the
thing. Where a beat says "Hale realizes X", do not have him announce X — let the
reader feel him arrive at it. Where two people speak, let them speak like people.
Hit every beat in order, keep the scene ids and the branch structure exactly
(they matter to how the book is read), but inside each scene the craft is yours.

This is a mystery, so it lives on what is *not yet said*. The three big secrets
(marked WITHHOLD below) are the engines of the book: keep them genuinely unknown
until their scenes — never let the narrator or a character state the solution
early. A good mystery never tips its hand; let the planted objects carry the
suspicion and let the reader lean forward. That restraint is not a constraint on
your warmth — it *is* the warmth of suspense. Conversely, the two things the
reader is let in on early (marked the READER SEES) are dramatic irony: show them
as witnessed objects/moments so the reader knows what Hale does not, and aches at
the gap.

## 2. The world and the people

A remote hilltop observatory — the Vane Observatory — snow-cut from the valley
for the winter of 1898, only its small resident staff on the summit. At dawn the
director, **Dr. Aurel Crane** (sixty, blind these two years, a proud and private
man, offstage — we meet him only as a body, an unsent letter, a square old hand
in a log), is found dead at the foot of the iron stair beneath the great
refractor, the dome shutter frozen open, the meridian clock stopped at 3:14. A
patron sends **Lewin Hale**, a methodical insurance adjuster, to certify whether
the instrument policy pays for an accident — or for something else. Hale has
crouched over a hundred deaths other men called accidents; he is kind in a worn,
unshowy way, and he distrusts his own kindness most of all.

- **Junia Frost** — assistant astronomer. Was on the dome gallery the death-night
  and saw who climbed the iron stair; she lies (says she was at the spectroscope)
  to shield them. Grief and a fierce private code drive her. She wrote the
  death-night log in her own hand — at blind Crane's dictation, a kindness — but
  the reader and Hale first read that hand as a forger's.
- **Onslow Pike** — bursar. Restless hands, a warm room, a soft place in the
  ledgers. He drained the instrument account for two years and forged a patron's
  telegram recalling Crane to dismissal, to be rid of him before the books were
  read. He believes, with a whole heart, that he killed Crane — and carries that
  certainty like a stone.

## 3. What the reader is let in on early (dramatic irony — SHOW, don't tell)

- **THE READER SEES (early): a wrong was done with money and a cruel recall.**
  In the opening, a half-burnt telegram form survives in the dead grate
  — *…patron… recall… without delay…* — and Hale's eye passes over it without
  stooping. Later, Pike's restless guilt around the ledgers and the unsent
  resignation. The reader should sense, ahead of Hale, that the bursar is rotten
  with something. (Do NOT yet let the reader see the *resolution* — see WITHHOLD
  W3.)
- **THE READER SEES (early): Junia saw the climber and is lying.** Scene 2 is
  *hers* — the reader stands on the gallery with her memory, knows she saw a
  shape climb, knows she has chosen to lie. The reader must NOT be told *who* she
  saw (that is WITHHOLD W1) — only that she saw, and lies, and why it costs her.

## 4. The three withheld secrets (the mysteries — keep them shut until their scenes)

- **WITHHOLD W1 — who climbed the iron stair.** The reader and Hale must not know
  until the reveal. Never name the climber, in narration or dialogue, before:
  the REVEAL ending (scene sc-17r, Junia names him) — and in the QUIET-AUDIT road
  he is never named aloud at all, only reasoned cold from physical proof at the
  end. In the BURN ending he is never named, ever.
- **WITHHOLD W2 — that it was no murder; the clock at 3:14 is the jolt of the
  fall, not a planted alibi.** Hold the question "was this killing?" open. The
  truth (an accidental startle-fall; the clock *struck*, not set) surfaces only
  through Hale's own physical reasoning at its scenes (sc-15 the clock; sc-17r /
  sc-18b the death), never as an early narrator statement.
- **WITHHOLD W3 — what really became of the forged recall, and the proven shape
  of the theft.** The reader is let to *suspect* money-wrong early (§3); but the
  *resolution* — that the telegram lay undelivered the whole time so Crane never
  knew, and the theft reconstructed and proven — must wait for its scenes
  (sc-11a / sc-19b; the audit road sc-09b–12b for the reconstruction).

## 5. The scene map (hit every beat, in order; keep the ids and forks exactly)

Focal character in brackets. Each scene 150–400 words. Total around 9,400 words.

### The shared opening — sc-01 … sc-08

- **sc-01 [Hale].** Crane's body folded at the foot of the iron stair; angle and
  the railing-bruise say he fell from the top. A blind man, sixty, on a dark
  stair at 3 a.m. — the policy would call it nothing stranger, and the policy is
  usually right. *Plant (READER SEES):* the curl of unburnt telegram in the dead
  grate, *…patron… recall… without delay…*; Hale's eye passes over it, he does
  not stoop. He writes *fall*.
- **sc-02 [Junia].** She watches Hale's trap come up the road, alibi ready ("the
  spectroscope, all night"). Her mind climbs the stair against her will: the
  gallery, the cold in her teeth, a shape coming in the lower door and going up —
  she knew the tread, the height of the shoulders. She has decided to lie to
  shield them. *Plant:* the maid's dawn crossing left a second set of prints in
  the frost over the first. (WITHHOLD W1: never name who she saw.)
- **sc-03 [Hale].** The night-log's last entry — death-night — is in a wrong
  hand: younger, quicker, modern loops, not Crane's square script. A second
  presence in the dome that night, steady enough to write stars while a man fell.
  He writes *Who?* (The innocent truth — her hand at dictation — is WITHHELD to
  sc-16/sc-18b.)
- **sc-04 [Hale].** The plate-camera: Junia said she worked the instrument all
  night. The plate is *blank* — unexposed, never drawn to the sky. He notes it,
  but out of kindness lets a grieving witness stand, not a suspect. (Payoff
  withheld to sc-14/sc-17b.)
- **sc-05 [Hale].** The meridian clock stopped at 3:14, lately wound (weight
  high, no slack). He sees the clock-case is bolted into the same iron frame as
  the stair-foot — pier, stair, clock one rigid skeleton — and flinches from the
  too-neat thought of a blow ringing the pendulum. Writes *clock stopped 3:14,
  lately wound*. (WITHHOLD W2: do not yet resolve struck-vs-set.)
- **sc-06 [Hale].** Pike's warm room; Hale comes for the instrument-account
  valuation. Pike over-eager, hands never still; a *soft place* in the figures.
  Under the blotter, Crane's unsent resignation in his own square hand — *my
  sight being now wholly gone* — private despair, never sent. Pike goes
  snow-white to see it read. *Plants:* the drained account (motive); the unsent
  letter.
- **sc-07 [Hale].** The key board: two bare hooks. Pike says the spare *dome*
  key (lower door, foot of the stair) was lost a month ago; the day-book confirms
  it, a month old, in Pike's hand. Hale dislikes the neat interval. (The truth —
  Pike kept it — is WITHHELD to sc-11b/sc-18r.)
- **sc-08 [Hale].** Third reading of the account: the soft place hardens into a
  drained sum — phantom freight, fittings never received, two years of it, dressed
  in dry bookkeeping. A motive lies open. He weighs the lit strip under the
  bursar's door. **FORK-1.** Write a `### CHOICE` card: **CONFRONT** (lay the
  ledger before Pike) → sc-09a, or **AUDIT QUIETLY** (reconstruct it unseen) →
  sc-09b.

### CONFRONT limb — sc-09a … sc-16

- **sc-09a [Hale].** Hale lays the open ledger before Pike; Pike collapses and
  confesses — the theft, guinea by guinea; *and* that he forged the patron's
  recall to be rid of Crane before the books were read. Hale's theory goes hard
  and certain: Pike drove the old man to that stair. (Hale now *believes*, wrongly,
  that Pike caused the death.)
- **sc-10a [Hale].** He lets Pike weep. But, distrustful of an easy confession,
  Hale asks his ugly question — *did it happen the way the guilty man believes?*
  He has seen but one corner of that telegram (the char in the grate). He resolves
  to go down the hill and find it whole.
- **sc-11a [Hale].** The post-box at the foot of the road, snowed in a clean
  month. The telegram is there — franked, undelivered, never carried the last
  mile. **Crane never read it, never knew of the recall.** With the unsent
  resignation, Hale reads them together: Crane meant to leave of his own grief,
  his own failing eyes — no plot reached him. His certainty cracks: if Crane never
  knew, Pike confessed to a death he never caused. The fall stands unexplained.
  (W3 resolution begins; W2 opens — no one drove him.)
- **sc-12a [Pike].** Alone, certain he is a murderer. He is *sure* he sent the
  wire (he never learned it failed). His grief is real; he confessed because in
  his own accounting the killing is the true thing and the money only money. He
  has buried the duplicate books and kept the key warm in his coat-lining all
  month, like a man hiding a weapon. (Dramatic irony: the reader now doubts the
  wire ever arrived; Pike never will.)
- **sc-13 [Hale].** Back up the hill, he goes to the *dome*, not the warm room.
  Set the confession aside; set the telegram aside. What did he catalogue and not
  read? The blank plate, the stopped clock, the strange hand. He resolves to go
  back to each and leave none of them.
- **sc-14 [Hale].** He develops the blank plate himself, the slow cold way, and
  watches nothing rise — no star, never exposed. So Junia was *not* at the
  instrument; her sober account is air. The witness he spared turns, in his book,
  into the liar he failed to hear.
- **sc-15 [Hale].** The clock again — but now he reads the iron beneath, not the
  brass above. Clock-case and stair-foot are one bone of metal. A fall striking
  the stair would ring straight through to the brittle pendulum. So the clock was
  **struck, not set**: 3:14 is the recorded instant of the fall itself, not a
  planted alibi. He writes the hour again, not as a question now but as a fact.
  (W2 half-resolved.)
- **sc-16 [Hale].** He brings the wet plate to Junia. She gives the *small* truth:
  the night-log is her hand — she wrote the figures at blind Crane's dictation,
  his fingers too stiff to hold the pen; a kindness, not a forgery. Hale lets the
  forger-suspicion go. But a larger truth — who climbed — still sits behind her
  teeth. **FORK-2.** Write a `### CHOICE` card: **REVEAL** (she names what she
  saw) → sc-17r, or **BURN** (she feeds the log to the stove) → sc-17w.

### REVEAL ending — sc-17r … sc-19r

- **sc-17r [Junia→Hale].** She names the climber at last: **it was Pike** on the
  stair — she knew his shoulders against the dome-light, his soft tread; he had a
  key; he went up into the dark where Crane was. She has known a fortnight. *But
  it was no murder*: Crane was already on the stair, sleepless over his own grief
  and failing eyes; the fall was the startle of a tread where none should be. Pike
  never laid a hand on him — "he climbed a thief and came down having killed a man
  he never touched." She lied to keep a thief from hanging for an unmeant death.
  (W1 + W2 resolved — through her voice, dramatized.)
- **sc-18r [Hale].** Everything turns and faces one way. The key was never lost —
  kept warm in Pike's coat a month, it let him into the sealed dome unseen the
  death-night. The blank plate — she was on the gallery, watching. The struck
  clock. The wire that never climbed the hill. The old man already on the dark
  stair of his own sorrow. Pike came up a thief to lift the duplicate books and
  startled a blind man off the iron; no soul planned a death. He holds it whole.
- **sc-19r [Hale].** The certificate, chosen line by line. Not *murder* (no hand
  was laid on Crane), though Pike in the warm room would almost welcome the word.
  But not the clean accident the policy pays, either: a man let himself by stealth
  into a sealed dome on an errand of theft and a frail blind director died of the
  intrusion. *A fall, occasioned by unlawful intrusion; not accidental within the
  meaning of the policy; no malice toward the person of the deceased established.*
  Pike answers for theft, forgery, breaking-in — not the rope. (Ending W1.)

### BURN ending — sc-17w … sc-18w

- **sc-17w [Hale].** She does not answer. She lifts the night-log and feeds it to
  the workroom stove; Hale understands a half-second too late. He does not wrestle
  a grieving woman over a stove. The log goes up all at once — and with it the one
  record that could have proved her hand innocent *and* the only thread from the
  gallery to the climber's name. Her stillness is the spent calm of someone who
  paid a price on purpose. The summit will keep its dead man's secret. (W1
  withheld forever — never named.)
- **sc-18w [Hale].** The hardest certificate he ever signed. The fraud he has
  cold — drained account, the forged recall found undelivered proving Pike's hand
  at the wire. The key he can write only as a *means without a man*: opportunity
  of unobserved entry by the spare key reported lost a month prior. But the death:
  no witness, no record, suspicion not proof — he must write *death by fall; cause
  undetermined; no unlawful act against the deceased established.* Accidental,
  unproven otherwise — so the policy pays, on a death he alone is sure was not
  clean. The proof is ash in a stove. (Ending W2.)

### QUIET-AUDIT limb — sc-09b … sc-12b, then sc-17b … sc-20b

- **sc-09b [Hale].** He does not cross the station. He closes his book softly and
  turns back to the ledgers — the slow road: reconstruct the theft from the page
  alone while Pike believes himself unseen, and *watch* what an unsuspected man
  does in the dark. He builds the drained shape guinea by guinea, in silence.
- **sc-10b [Hale].** From the black of the upper landing he keeps watch. Past
  midnight Pike's door opens without a lamp; Pike comes out stooped, the duplicate
  books against his chest, patting something low at his coat-lining — a man moving
  as if burying a weapon, believing himself far worse than a thief. Hale does not
  stir.
- **sc-11b [Hale].** Pike goes to the lower dome door — the locked one at the foot
  of the stair, whose key has been "lost" a month — and draws from his coat-lining
  *the key*. The lost key was never lost; it lay warm against him the whole month.
  He lets himself in. Hale's slow theory goes hard: a hidden way in, kept secret,
  used by a man who believes himself a murderer. Not an accident. (Hale witnesses
  the *means*; he still never has the climber *named* — keep W1 a reasoned
  inference here, never an eyewitness naming.)
- **sc-12b [Hale].** He follows Pike down, soft on the iron, and watches him bury
  the duplicate books in the foundations. He does not stop him — a watched truth
  is worth more than a quarrel in the dark. He has the theft proved, the lost key
  found warm and fitted to the death-door, a bursar who creeps by night. He holds
  it whole and waits for the morning.
- **sc-17b [Hale].** (Morning.) Alone, he develops the blank plate first — nothing
  rises, never exposed. Junia was not at the instrument; she lied. A person does
  not lie so for nothing.
- **sc-18b [Hale].** He makes the clock and the log speak for themselves. The
  clock-case is one bone with the stair-frame; 3:14 is the struck instant of the
  fall, not an alibi. The strange hand in the log is Junia writing at blind
  Crane's dictation — a kindness reasoned out of his blindness and the cold, no
  forger and no killer; he sets the phantom down without asking her to lift it.
  (W2 resolved by reasoning; the log cleared.)
- **sc-19b [Hale].** The loose papers Pike freely handed over hold the copy-book
  draft of the forged recall in Pike's own square forgery; from it Hale traces the
  wire down to the post-box — franked, snowed-in, *undelivered*. Crane never read
  it, never knew. With the unsent resignation: the old man meant to lay down his
  post of his own grief; no plot reached him. He was on a dark stair over a private
  sorrow when a thief came soft through a door that should have been locked. The
  whole assembles — every part from a thing seen or read, never from a confession.
  (W3 resolution; W1 reasoned, not named.)
- **sc-20b [Hale].** The certificate, in daylight. He never raised his voice;
  he built the case from objects that cannot be coaxed or broken or made to weep —
  the clock that timed its own death, the plate that broke her alibi, the wire that
  never climbed, the key gone warm a month early. No confession in it. *A fall,
  occasioned by the deceased being startled by an unlawful and unobserved
  intrusion; not an accident within the meaning of the policy; no malice shown.*
  Pike answers for theft, forgery, breaking-in. The worth of the slow road: nothing
  a frightened tongue can take back. (Ending W3.)

## 6. Deliverable format (exact — the book is read by these rules)

- Plain markdown to the file the task names. A short title/frame paragraph up
  top is fine.
- Every scene under a heading `## sc-01`, `## sc-02`, … using the EXACT ids
  above (`## sc-09a`, `## sc-17r`, `## sc-17w`, `## sc-09b`, `## sc-20b`, etc.).
  You may add a short title after the id (e.g. `## sc-01 — The Foot of the Iron
  Stair`) but the id must be first and exact.
- At each fork, a `### CHOICE` card naming the two options and the scene each
  continues to (exactly as in §5). Mark the two fork limbs and the three endings
  clearly with brief bracket notes between scenes if helpful (e.g. `[CONFRONT
  limb. Continues from sc-08, CHOICE A.]`).
- End with a short `## World-line and ending map` listing the three readable
  paths: CONFRONT→REVEAL (sc-01…08, sc-09a…16, sc-17r…19r); CONFRONT→BURN
  (…sc-17w, sc-18w); QUIET-AUDIT (sc-01…08, sc-09b…12b, sc-17b…20b).
- 29 scenes total. 150–400 words each, ~9,400 words overall. Write them in id
  order. If you must stop partway, stop at a scene boundary and say where you
  stopped.

Write the whole thing as one continuous authorial voice. Make it a story someone
will not want to put down.
