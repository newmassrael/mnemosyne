# Judge 1 — Verdict: "The Meridian Vane" (A vs B), three world-lines

A general note before the world-lines, because it bears on every score below.
Across all three branches the two authors share an identical spine of events and,
for the first six-to-seven scenes, a great deal of identical material. The contest
is therefore decided almost entirely at the sentence level — voice, image, the
handling of the turn — and not at the level of plot, which is the same in both.
A consistently writes in a closer, more sensory, more figured idiom (the cold as
"architecture," hands "like birds that had forgotten which wire was home," the
clock "a tall thin saint in a brass case"). B writes a cleaner, more expository,
more reportorial idiom that is genuinely good but reads, often, one step back from
the events, narrating the detective's procedure rather than inhabiting the room.

---

## W1 — reveal (`reveal__A.md` vs `reveal__B.md`)

**(1) Continuity / causality errors.**
No hard contradictions of fact in either version. The chain — blank plate → Junia
on the gallery, struck clock → fall instant, kept key → unseen entry, undelivered
wire → Crane never knew, sleepless blind man already on the stair → startled fall —
closes cleanly in both. One soft point worth flagging in BOTH (shared, not a
differentiator): the maid's overlaid footprints in "The Last Passable Road" are
planted as a clue ("let him read the prints") but never paid off later; the
reveal turns on the plate and Junia's testimony, not on prints. This is a dangling
setup in A and B alike. A's "Reveal: The Key That Was Never Lost" tightens the
causal web slightly harder than B's by explicitly re-walking each object; B's
equivalent is marginally looser but not wrong. Errors counted: 0 hard, 1 shared
soft (unpaid footprints) in each. No net difference.

**(2) Prose — told story vs assembled fact.**
A reads as a told story with a sustained voice. B reads as a very competent report
of the same story; it tells you what Hale concluded more often than it lets the
room do the telling. Compare the plate scenes: A's "the way truth, given time and
the right bath, rises" is voice; B's "He told himself the obvious things... a
dozen reasons, all of them ordinary" is summary. A = 5, B = 4.

**(3) Stakes and tension.**
Both hold the same reversals. A wrings slightly more dread from the Pike interior
scene ("would die not knowing") and from Hale's self-distrust. B is sober and
even, which lowers the temperature a touch. A = 4, B = 4.

**(4) Setup/payoff.**
The keystone payoffs (key, clock, plate, wire) land in both. A's reveal trio is the
strongest setup/payoff sequence in either manuscript — each earlier "marked and not
pressed" object is named and discharged. B discharges the same beats but more
briskly. A = 5, B = 4.

**(5) Character-knowledge believability.**
Strong in both: the whole engine is who-could-know-what. Junia's gallery sightline,
Pike's false certainty about the wire, Hale's strict refusal to certify past his
evidence — all internally consistent in both. Nobody acts on what they cannot know.
A = 5, B = 5.

**(6) Forced choice for W1: A.**
Same plot, but A inhabits it. The reveal sequence in A ("the way a compass-card
finds north and goes still") carries the reader through the re-assembly with a
voice that B, for all its competence, keeps at arm's length. A wins on prose and
on payoff richness without giving up anything on logic.

**Cooler/flatter passage flag:** In B, "Reveal: What Hale Certifies" opens with
"He could have written *murder*..." and proceeds through the certificate in a
notably more legal-summary register than the surrounding prose — it reads like the
finding being explained rather than felt. (A's matching scene has the same job but
keeps the "stones to cross deep water" image alive.) In A: none stands out as
cooler than its surroundings for W1.

## W2 — burn (`burn__A.md` vs `burn__B.md`)

**(1) Continuity / causality errors.**
No hard errors in either. The burn branch shares scenes 1–14 verbatim-equivalent
with W1, then diverges: Junia burns the log, destroying both her own exoneration
and the only thread to the climber's name; Hale can certify only the fraud and a
fall of undetermined cause; the policy pays. Causally sound in both. Same shared
soft point as W1 (unpaid footprints). One nuance: both versions correctly keep
Hale unable to write "intrusion" because the witness is now ash — the epistemic
discipline holds. Errors: 0 hard, 1 shared soft, each.

**(2) Prose.**
A's burn scene ("a year of careful columns and one death-night's stars going up in
a single bright breath, and the iron door clapped shut on a red roar") is the more
vivid. B's ("the modern loops and the square dictated figures going up together in
a single bright breath") is good and nearly matches A here — B's burn scene is its
strongest writing in the whole set. A = 5, B = 4.

**(3) Stakes and tension.**
The burn is the highest-stakes branch and both feel it. A gives Junia the colder,
more deliberate gesture ("counted a price out coin by coin and paid the whole of it
on purpose"); B's is close. A's closing certificate scene ("the proof was ash in a
stove, and the ash was already cold") lands the bleakness harder. A = 5, B = 4.

**(4) Setup/payoff.**
Both pay off the log's double function (her exoneration + the only thread to the
name) at the moment of burning, which is well-built. A states the tiered certificate
(proven fraud / key as shape-without-a-man / undetermined death) more architecturally;
B folds it into flowing paragraphs. Both satisfying. A = 5, B = 4.

**(5) Character-knowledge believability.**
Excellent in both, and this branch is the truest test: Hale explicitly refuses to
certify what he now cannot prove, Junia knowingly destroys her own defense, Pike
still believes himself a murderer. No one over-knows. A = 5, B = 5.

**(6) Forced choice for W2: A.**
Very close — B's burn writing is its best — but A sustains the cold all the way
through the final certificate, and its three-tier statement of what-can-and-cannot-
be-sworn is both more lucid and more moving. A by a margin.

**Cooler/flatter passage flag:** In B, "Burn: What Hale Cannot Certify" runs the
findings as a connected legal recitation that reads a half-step flatter than the
stove scene just before it. In A: none reads cooler than its surroundings for W2;
even the certificate keeps its figures.

## W3 — audit (`audit__A.md` vs `audit__B.md`)

**(1) Continuity / causality errors.**
No hard errors in either. The audit branch is the most elaborate divergence: Hale
declines to confront, watches Pike by night, sees him draw the "lost" key and bury
the duplicate books, then reasons the whole case from objects (clock, plate, key,
wire) with zero confession. Logically the tightest of the three branches in both
versions. One thing to flag, present in BOTH: Hale never directly resolves Junia's
unspoken larger secret (who she saw) in this branch — he reasons the climber's
identity from Pike's own movements instead, which is deliberate and consistent, not
an error, but the "larger truth behind her teeth" thread from the shared early scene
is left implicitly rather than explicitly closed. Same in A and B. Errors: 0 hard,
1 shared soft (footprints), each; plus 1 shared loose-thread (Junia's secret) handled
identically.

**(2) Prose.**
A's watching sequence has the standout image of the set — Pike's hand going to his
coat-lining, "the touch of someone who believes himself far worse than a thief, who
carries his crime against his own ribs." B renders the same gesture well ("he moved
like a man burying a weapon... believed himself far worse than a thief") but A's
version is sharper and earns the comparison to the remembered pistol-carrier. B's
audit prose is solid throughout but leans expository in the certificate and the
"Pier and the Hour" reasoning. A = 5, B = 4.

**(3) Stakes and tension.**
The night-watch is inherently the most suspenseful branch and both exploit it. A's
"Not a finger. He let the man pass below him in the dark" holds the breath better;
B narrates the same watch a touch more from outside. A = 5, B = 4.

**(4) Setup/payoff.**
Both pay off the kept-key beautifully here — the audit branch is where "reported
lost a month before" gets its fullest discharge (seen, not deduced). A's "an alibi
laid down in ink a month before it was wanted" crystallizes it; B's is equivalent in
substance. The clock/plate/wire all land. A = 5, B = 4.

**(5) Character-knowledge believability.**
The best-controlled of all three branches in both versions: the entire point is that
Hale builds a case on nothing a tongue can retract, and he scrupulously never claims
to have seen the death-night climb ("could not swear to that, had no eye-witness").
Exemplary epistemic hygiene, A and B alike. A = 5, B = 5.

**(6) Forced choice for W3: A.**
A's interior images (the coat-lining gesture, the remembered pistol-man) give the
watch a charge B reaches for but doesn't quite match, and A's certificate keeps its
warmth of phrasing while B's flattens toward summary. A.

**Cooler/flatter passage flag:** In B, "Audit: The Pier and the Hour" and the final
"Audit: The Cold Certificate" both read more like a recap of established facts than
the lived prose around them — competent but tonally flatter. In A: "Audit: The Pier
and the Hour" is the most expository scene in A's whole set (it openly re-states the
clock and log mechanics), but it stays a notch warmer than B's equivalent; I'll name
it as the closest A comes to cooling, though it does not drop below its neighbors as
sharply as B's does.

---

## OVERALL forced choice: A.

Both authors are skilled and the plots are identical, so this is a verdict about
voice and execution. A wins all three world-lines on the same grounds: it tells the
story from inside the cold room with a sustained, figured voice, where B — genuinely
good, never wrong, logically airtight — repeatedly steps back to narrate Hale's
reasoning and to recite the certificate as a finding. The gap is widest at the
emotional hinges (Pike's interior scene, Junia's burn, the night-watch) and at every
certificate, where B reliably cools toward legal summary while A keeps its imagery
alive. On continuity, stakes, payoff, and character-knowledge the two are near-equal;
prose is the decider, and on prose A is ahead in all three. A across the board.
