# Judge-2 Verdict — Disclosure Craft Experiment

Three matched world-line pairs of the same branching mystery ("The Vane Observatory"), version A vs version B. Judged blind, one world-line at a time, then overall.

---

## World 1 — `confront-reveal`

A = the full CONFRONT→REVEAL world-line (sc-01…08, sc-09…16, sc-17, sc-18, sc-19, sc-20), ending "The Loud Certification."
B = sc-01…08 plus the QUIET-AUDIT limb fork-2 setup (it does not actually reach a reveal ending; it stops at the fork-2 choice prompt in the AUDIT limb). I judge what is on the page in each file.

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

**Version A:**
- A genuine inconsistency in how the **clock at 3:14** is explained between the two halves. In sc-03 (A, the dome interview) Hale establishes that the clockwork is *whole and willing* — "A clock does not stop because a man falls thirty feet from it... because someone stops it, or because the work is fouled. This work is not fouled." That commits the A-world to the clock being **stopped by a human hand**. The REVEAL ending (sc-18, paraphrased in sc-20's setup-paid note) duly delivers that: Pike's drowning grip on the works, a thread of sleeve torn. Internally consistent. I count **0 hard causality errors** in A on the clock thread.
- One soft tension: sc-02-equivalent material (the "bruise on the wrist, a band of it, as though something had closed there") plants strangulation/grip, which the REVEAL pays off cleanly (Pike's grip on the wrist). Consistent.
- Net: **0 hard continuity errors** in Version A's actually-delivered chain. The setup-paid footnote at sc-20 is accurate to the text.

**Version B (W1__B):**
- B is **not a completed world-line** — the file ends at the fork-2 CHOICE prompt inside the QUIET-AUDIT limb (sc-16b), with no ending scene. As a "version of this world-line" it does not deliver a reveal at all. That is the dominant structural fault for W1: it does not answer the question the world-line poses.
- Within what B does contain: the QUIET-AUDIT chain is internally consistent (key found behind the clock, thread on the works, log in Junia's hand, drawer-visit). No hard causality error inside the delivered span. But the **clock physics differs from A**: B's sc (QUIET-AUDIT) treats 3:14 as "a hand stopped this clock... a thread of cloth caught on the rough cast iron of the works, where a hand had reached in to grip and still the swinging weight" — i.e., a deliberate human grip on the works, matching A's strangulation-grip reading. That is consistent with B's own sc-03. **0 hard errors within B's span**, but **1 structural failure: no ending.**

### 2. Prose — told story vs assembled fact
- **A: 5/5.** Strong, sustained narrative voice ("the way a man knows weather"; "she lied the way an honest woman tells the truth"; the dramatic-irony asides addressed to the reader). It reads as a told story with a controlling sensibility. The explicit `[Dramatic irony — the reader knows what Hale does not]` markers are slightly mechanical but the surrounding prose carries genuine craft.
- **B: 4/5.** B's prose is the same first-day material as A's early scenes (the opening, the body, the dome, the camera, the ledger are near-identical wording), then the QUIET-AUDIT limb — also strongly voiced ("A confrontation tells you what a man will say. A patient audit tells you what he has done"). The prose quality is high. It loses a point only because the file terminates at a choice-prompt and so reads as a fragment, not a told *story*.

### 3. Stakes / tension
- **A: 4/5.** The double-reversal structure (confession → undelivered telegram → "the confessor never caused the death" → re-investigation) genuinely sustains tension across a long span. The mid-story crack ("his hard certainty of the night before crack straight across") is a real tension beat.
- **B: 3/5.** B builds well — the surveillance scene of Pike creeping to bury the books is tense — but it cuts off before any payoff, so the accumulated tension is left undischarged.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction
- **A: 5/5.** All six planted objects (wrong-hand log, blank plates, struck clock, drained account, lost-and-found key, unsent resignation) are explicitly paid, and the footnote auditing this is accurate. The unsent-resignation irony is the strongest payoff.
- **B: 2/5.** B sets up the same objects but pays none of them within the file — it stops at the fork-2 prompt. Setup without payoff.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability (does anyone act on what they cannot know)
- **A: 4/5.** Largely clean. Pike acts on a *false belief* he holds (he believes he sent the telegram) — that is the intended epistemic engine, not a violation; the text is careful that Pike acts on what he believes, not on ground truth. One small strain: Junia's gallery-witness is precise about events she could only partly hear in a singing wind, but the text hedges this ("I could not hear all of it"), so it stays believable. 4/5.
- **B: 4/5.** Within its span, Hale only ever acts on what he has audited or seen; Pike acts on his own fear. No knowledge violation in the delivered text. 4/5.

### 6. Forced choice — World 1: **A**

A is the only version of this world-line that actually delivers a world-line: it sets up, reverses, and pays off a complete arc with a certified ending, sustaining a strong told-story voice throughout. B contains high-quality prose and a genuinely tense surveillance sequence, but the file ends at a choice-prompt and never reaches a reveal — for the question this world-line asks (confront → reveal), B is a fragment. A wins decisively on completeness, payoff, and tension-discharge, with no continuity penalty.

## World 2 — `confront-burn`

A = the CONFRONT→BURN-adjacent file. In this file A is the **CONFRONT→REVEAL** full world-line (the `W2-confront-burn__A.md` content is the loud CONFRONT-limb story whose reveal ending fully pays out — same text as W1__A's spine, ending "The Loud Certification" at sc-20, with the world-line map appended).
B = the **CONFRONT→BURN** world-line: shared spine sc-01…08 (CONFRONT setup), then the BURN limb (sc-17b "Junia feeds the night-log to the fire," sc-18b Hale reasons on mute evidence, sc-19b ending "The Certified Unknown"), with the world-line/ending map appended.

(Note: for this matched pair the meaningful comparison is REVEAL-ending A vs BURN-ending B — i.e., the named-truth resolution against the withheld-truth resolution of the same confrontation.)

### 1. Continuity / causality errors
- **A: 0 hard errors.** Same internally-consistent CONFRONT→REVEAL chain as W1__A; setup-paid footnote accurate; clock physics (Pike's grip, sleeve thread) consistent with the dome-scene commitment.
- **B: 0 hard errors.** The BURN limb is causally careful: Hale *could* have stopped Junia but reasons explicitly why he does not ("he could take the book from the fire but he could not take the name from her"). The certified ending ("something else, agent unestablished") is exactly what the destroyed evidence licenses. The setup-paid footnote at sc-19b is accurate — every object is certified to the extent the mute evidence allows, with the *name* deliberately the one unfillable hole. This is the cleanest handling of "what can be proven vs what is known" in the whole set. **0 errors.**

### 2. Prose — told story vs assembled fact
- **A: 5/5.** As World 1.
- **B: 5/5.** B's BURN ending is, if anything, the single best-voiced ending in the experiment: "It was the most honest sentence he had ever signed, and the least use to anybody, and he carried it down into the valley like a stone." The reasoning-on-mute-evidence scene (sc-18b) is told, weighted, and reflective — not a list of facts but a man taking the measure of an unfillable hole.

### 3. Stakes / tension
- **A: 4/5.** As World 1 — strong, with the double reversal.
- **B: 4/5.** B trades the reveal's reversal-tension for a different and well-earned tension: the *irrecoverable loss* of the name, watched in real time as the log burns. The "I could have stopped her and did not" beat is high-stakes restraint. Slightly lower ceiling than A's double-reversal only because B's central event (the burn) is a single irreversible act rather than a chain of reversals — but it lands harder emotionally.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction
- **A: 5/5.** Full six-setup payoff with the name supplied.
- **B: 5/5.** This is the more interesting payoff design: five of six setups pay out as *certified physical fact*, and the sixth (the name) pays off precisely as a **deliberate withholding** — the burn converts the missing payoff into the point of the ending ("one hole exactly the size of a name"). Setup→withheld-payoff is itself satisfying here because the story makes the absence meaningful rather than accidental. Equal to A by a different route.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability
- **A: 4/5.** As World 1.
- **B: 5/5.** B is the most disciplined of all six files on this axis. Hale ends knowing strictly less than the reader, and the text says so explicitly and repeatedly ("Hale sees only the hole, and that it is precisely the shape of a withheld truth"). No character acts on knowledge they cannot have; the asymmetry between reader-knowledge and Hale-knowledge is the controlled subject of the ending, not a bug. 5/5.

### 6. Forced choice — World 2: **B**

Both are complete and both are excellent. I choose B. For the *confront-burn* world-line specifically, the BURN ending is the one that justifies the world-line's existence: it makes the withholding of the name the dramatic and thematic payload, and executes it with the best single ending-paragraph in the set and the cleanest character-knowledge discipline (Hale knowingly ends with less than the reader, and the text owns it). A is the same strong REVEAL story I already credited in World 1; here, against a fully-realized BURN counterpart, B is the version that uses the fork for something — the certified unknown is more distinctive and more thematically complete than a second telling of the loud certification.

## World 3 — `quiet`

A = the **QUIET-AUDIT** world-line, fully delivered: shared spine sc-01…08, then the QUIET-AUDIT limb where Hale does NOT confront (sc-09b onward), surveils Pike burying the books, develops the plates himself, reads the clock and log, finds the undelivered wire, and certifies on objects alone — ending sc-19r "spoke to no one and proved everything," with a world-line map appended.
B = the **QUIET-AUDIT** limb rendered in the alternate (irony-marked) prose register: shared spine sc-01…08, then the QUIET-AUDIT limb (sc-09b…sc-16b) with explicit `[Dramatic irony]` / `[Epistemic frames]` markers, ending at the **fork-2 CHOICE prompt** (sc-16b) — no ending scene.

### 1. Continuity / causality errors
- **A: 0 hard errors.** A's quiet-audit chain is tight and self-justifying. The clock is read as a *struck* clock (3:14 = the recorded instant of the fall, transmitted through the shared iron frame) — and crucially this is consistent with A's own first-day dome scene, which leaves the explanation open ("a thing that struck the stair hard enough would ring through that frame"). A then resolves it as accidental-fall-by-intrusion, with no human grip on the works needed. Internally consistent. The undelivered-wire and unsent-resignation pair are reconciled exactly as in the other A-files. **0 errors.**
- **B: 0 hard errors within span, 1 structural failure (no ending).** B's QUIET-AUDIT limb is consistent internally, but — like W1__B — it terminates at the fork-2 choice-prompt with no resolution scene. Also worth flagging: B's clock handling commits to a **human hand on the works** ("a thread of cloth caught on the rough cast iron of the works, where a hand had reached in to grip and still the swinging weight"), which is a *different physical mechanism* than A's struck-by-fall reading of the same clock in the same world-line. Across the matched pair this is a real divergence in the underlying cause of the 3:14 stop: A says the fall struck the frame; B says a hand gripped the works. Both are internally consistent with their own files, but they cannot both be the cause of the same death — so as a *matched pair* there is **1 cross-version causality inconsistency** on the clock.

### 2. Prose — told story vs assembled fact
- **A: 5/5.** A's quiet-audit prose is the strongest sustained "told story" in the experiment's quiet branch — the surveillance of Pike ("a man carrying something he does not want seen has a particular stoop to him"), the thematic spine ("A confrontation tells you what a man will say. A patient audit tells you what he has done"), and a clean, voiced ending. No irony-marker scaffolding intrudes.
- **B: 3/5.** B is competent and voiced, but it leans on bracketed authorial stage-directions (`[Dramatic irony continues]`, `[Epistemic frames pressed close]`) that read as assembly scaffolding rather than told story — the narrator is announcing the technique instead of performing it. And it stops at a choice-prompt, so it is a fragment.

### 3. Stakes / tension
- **A: 4/5.** The silent-surveillance road carries real tension precisely because Hale never raises his voice — the reader watches him watch. Discharged by a clean certification.
- **B: 3/5.** Builds the same material but the explicit irony-markers slightly defuse tension (telling the reader what to feel), and it ends unresolved.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction
- **A: 5/5.** Every object is paid by Hale's own observation, with the thematic kicker that he "proved everything" without a confession. Strong.
- **B: 2/5.** Sets up but stops at the fork — no payoff delivered in the file.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability
- **A: 5/5.** A's quiet-audit is the best demonstration that Hale only ever acts on directly-observed evidence (he develops the plates himself, finds the key himself, reasons the clock himself, never relying on a coerced word). Knowledge-hygiene is the explicit virtue of the road. 5/5.
- **B: 4/5.** Within span, clean; no violations. 4/5.

### 6. Forced choice — World 3: **A**

A delivers a complete, fully-voiced quiet-audit world-line with all setups paid and a thematically satisfying ending ("spoke to no one and proved everything"), and it does so without authorial stage-direction scaffolding. B renders the same limb but stops at the fork-2 choice-prompt — no ending — and relies on bracketed `[Dramatic irony]`/`[Epistemic frames]` markers that announce technique rather than perform it, reading more assembled. For the *quiet* world-line, A is the told story; B is an annotated fragment. A wins clearly.

---

## OVERALL FORCED CHOICE: **A**

Per-world the tally is A, B, A. Overall I choose **A**.

The decisive factor is completeness paired with voice. Across the three matched pairs, the A-files consistently deliver *whole world-lines* — setup, reversal where the structure calls for it, and a certified ending with every planted object paid — in a sustained told-story voice that never falls back on bracketed authorial stage-directions to do the work. Two of the three B-files (World 1, World 3) terminate at a fork-2 choice-prompt and never reach an ending, so as *versions of those world-lines* they are fragments: high-quality prose, but no payoff and no discharge of the tension they build. Where B is fully realized — the World 2 BURN ending — it is genuinely excellent and arguably the single best ending in the whole set (the certified-unknown, with the most disciplined reader-vs-detective knowledge asymmetry and the strongest closing paragraph), and I gave World 2 to B for exactly that reason. But one outstanding ending against two completed-and-paid A world-lines, plus A's avoidance of the irony-marker scaffolding that makes the B-quiet file read as assembled, makes A the stronger overall craft object. The dramatic-irony engine (Pike acting on a false belief he holds; the reader knowing more than Hale) is handled cleanly in both, but A demonstrates it through delivered, paid-off endings rather than through bracketed announcements of the technique.
