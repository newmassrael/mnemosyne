# Judge 3 — Verdict

Two versions (A and B) of the same branching mystery, judged one matched
world-line at a time, then an overall forced choice. Scores are 1–5.

A note carried through all three world-lines: the two versions are
*different renderings of the same underlying material*, not different
plots. A is the leaner, withholding telling — it shows objects and lets
their meaning surface late, holds the reader at Hale's level of
knowledge, and tends to dramatize discovery. B is the fuller, more
exposed telling — it states reader-known secrets outright in flagged
"dramatic irony" scenes, labels its own scene functions in bracketed
stage directions, and writes its setups/payoffs out explicitly. The
core question across all three is which telling reads as a *told story*
and which reads as an *assembled, self-annotating* one.

---

## WORLD 1 — confront → reveal

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

- **A:** No hard continuity breaks found. The chain is internally
  consistent: the half-burnt wire in the grate (sc-01) is the same
  forged recall that turns up undelivered in the post-box (sc-11/A's
  CONFRONT limb), Junia's gallery vantage (sc-02) supports her later
  witness, the clock-as-struck logic (sc-15) is set up by the shared
  iron-frame description (sc-05). One *soft* tension: A's CONFRONT-limb
  ending (sc-19/QUIET text it converges into) does not actually deliver
  the named-shape reveal that fork-2 CHOICE A promises — the file's
  CONFRONT→REVEAL limb (sc-16, fork-2) is cut off at the choice card and
  the rendered continuation is the QUIET-AUDIT limb's reveal, where
  *Junia* names Pike. So the "who" is delivered, but the routing label
  is loose. Count: 0 causality errors, 1 structural/routing looseness.
- **B:** No hard continuity breaks. B is more explicit and therefore
  easier to audit: the wrist-bruise (sc-02) is paid off precisely by
  Junia's account of Pike gripping the wrist (sc-18), the forged-telegram
  drawer (sc-04 irony) is the drawer Pike's glance betrays (sc-10) and
  that Hale opens (sc-15), the clock-grip with the sleeve-thread (sc-18)
  is the mechanism. The closing setup-ledger footnote cross-checks all
  six setups against scene ids and they hold. Count: 0.

### 2. Prose quality — told story vs. assembled

- **A: 5.** This reads as a genuinely *narrated* story with a consistent
  cold, clerical-but-haunted voice ("let the cold do his counting for
  him"; "guinea by spooned guinea"; "a confession was a door, not a
  room"). The withholding is deliberate craft: the wire is shown
  half-burnt and walked past, and the reader feels the walking-past.
- **B: 3.** B is well-written sentence by sentence and shares much of the
  same DNA, but it is repeatedly *interrupted by its own machinery*.
  Bracketed labels — "[Dramatic irony — the reader knows what Hale does
  not.]", "[Epistemic frames in collision …]", "[CONFRONT limb. The
  spare key.]" — and direct apostrophes to "the reader" ("Remember that
  the reader knows this") pull it from told-story toward
  annotated-construction. The closing "[All six setups paid: …]" ledger
  is the clearest tell: a told story does not append its own answer key.

### 3. Stakes and tension

- **A: 4.** Tension is real and slow-burning; the reveal that the
  confession was to "a death the confessor had never caused" is a strong
  reversal. Slightly lower than it could be because A's confront-limb
  reveal is the part that got routed/cut.
- **B: 4.** B front-loads the irony, which trades suspense (will-Hale-
  find-it) for dramatic irony (we-watch-him-not-find-it). That is a
  legitimate, sustained source of tension — the wrist-bruise and the
  word "recall falling out like a tooth" land hard — but the explicit
  "the reader knows / Hale does not" framing slightly deflates the
  pressure by narrating the gap instead of letting it bite.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

- **A: 4.** Payoffs are satisfying and earned through Hale's re-reading
  of objects he had catalogued and dismissed; the structure of "go back
  to each thing I let lie" is itself a payoff engine.
- **B: 5.** B's payoffs are the most complete and precisely interlocked
  of any file here — every setup discharges with a named mechanism
  (clock = drowning grip + sleeve-thread; key = month-hidden means;
  resignation = the irony certified). The explicit ledger is artless,
  but the *payoffs themselves* are the tightest in the set.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

- **A: 5.** No one acts on what they cannot know. Pike's whole tragedy
  is that he believes a thing that is false (he sent a wire that never
  arrived); A makes that a feature, and Hale only ever reasons from
  things in his book.
- **B: 5.** Same underlying logic, executed with even more explicit care
  — B foregrounds that Pike *believed* Crane knew, and that the belief
  (not a fact Pike possessed) is what moved him. Junia's witness is
  consistent with her established vantage. Clean.

### 6. Forced choice for World 1: **A**

Both versions tell the same strong story, and B has marginally tighter
payoff bookkeeping. But the brief asks which reads as a *told story with
a voice* versus an *assembled statement of fact*, and on that axis World
1 is not close: A immerses, B annotates. B's bracketed scene-function
labels and its closing setup ledger are the literal signature of
"assembled," and its direct addresses to "the reader" repeatedly break
the fictive surface. A achieves the same clues and the same reversal
*through narration*, trusting the reader to feel the withheld telegram
rather than being told it is being withheld. World 1 to **A**.

## WORLD 2 — confront → burn

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

- **A:** Note: A's W2 file renders the CONFRONT → **QUIET-AUDIT** ending,
  not a confront→burn ending — its sc-08 fork goes to the AUDIT-QUIETLY
  text and its second half is the QUIET-AUDIT burn (Junia burns the log
  at the stove). So within A the "confront-burn" label and the rendered
  content diverge: the body is a quiet-audit→burn line. As a *story*,
  internally consistent: the burned log destroys both the exoneration of
  the second hand and the only thread to the climber, and Hale's final
  "cause undetermined; policy pays" certificate follows logically from
  the lost evidence. Count: 0 causality errors, 1 label/branch mismatch.
- **B:** B's W2 file is the BURN world-line proper (sc-17b → sc-18b →
  sc-19b "The Certified Unknown") and also carries the explicit world-
  line/ending map at the end. Internally consistent and, again,
  self-auditing: the six-setup footnote confirms each setup is certified
  even without a name. Count: 0.

### 2. Prose quality — told story vs. assembled

- **A: 5.** A's burn scene is the strongest single passage across all
  six files: "the modern loops and the square dictated figures going up
  together in a single bright breath," "the spent calm of a person who
  has paid a price on purpose and found it bearable." It is pure
  narration, no scaffolding, and the loss lands as felt loss.
- **B: 3.** B again writes well but with the machinery showing —
  "[BURN world-line. Junia chose to destroy the log …]", "[BURN world-
  line. ENDING.]", the "**ENDING — 'The Certified Unknown'**" header,
  and the closing setup ledger. The prose inside the brackets is good;
  the brackets themselves keep it in document-mode rather than story-mode.

### 3. Stakes and tension

- **A: 4.** The burn is irreversible and the cost is dramatized in real
  time (Hale choosing *not* to lunge for the stove); the final
  certificate ("the money went out, clean, on a death he alone was sure
  had not been clean at all") is a bleak of genuine bitterness.
- **B: 4.** B's version of the same beat is also strong — Hale not moving
  because "he could take the book from the fire but he could not take the
  name from her" is an excellent line — but the surrounding labels and
  the explicit "[the dramatic-irony window stands fully open …]"
  narration tell the tension rather than transmit it.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

- **A: 4.** Satisfying *because* it withholds resolution honestly: the
  key is read "as a means without a man," and the unprovable death is
  certified as exactly that. The payoff is the *absence* of a clean
  payoff, earned.
- **B: 5.** B is more complete and more explicit: "agent unestablished"
  is a sharp, principled landing, and the setup ledger confirms all six
  threads are accounted for even in the burn line. Tightest bookkeeping,
  least art in the bookkeeping.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

- **A: 5.** Hale certifies only what the surviving physical evidence
  supports and explicitly refuses to certify the shape he cannot show.
  Junia acts on what she alone knows; no one over-reaches their knowledge.
- **B: 5.** Same discipline, stated even more openly ("one hole exactly
  the size of a name"). Both are clean on this axis.

### 6. Forced choice for World 2: **A**

This is the world-line where A's advantage is widest. The burn is an
emotional, irreversible beat, and A renders it as lived experience —
the stove, the single bright breath, Hale's deliberate refusal to
wrestle a grieving woman — while B renders it as a well-built but
*announced* ending, complete with section header and answer-key
footnote. B's "agent unestablished" certificate is arguably the cleaner
piece of reasoning, but the brief weights *told-story voice*, and A's
burn scene is the best-voiced passage in the entire set. World 2 to **A**.

## WORLD 3 — quiet

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

- **A:** A renders the QUIET-AUDIT line in full: Hale declines to
  confront, watches Pike creep out by night with the duplicate books and
  the month-kept key, follows, then in daylight develops the blank
  plate, reads the struck clock and the dictated log, traces the
  undelivered wire, and certifies a fall-by-unlawful-intrusion. This is
  the most rigorously *causal* of all six files — every conclusion is
  tied to a thing Hale personally saw or proved, and the text foregrounds
  that ("not once to a word any frightened man had confessed"). One
  internal-map note: A's appended world-line map labels World 2 as
  "CONFRONT → BURN" while A's actual W2 file rendered a quiet-audit→burn
  body — a map/label inconsistency, not a story-causality error. Count:
  0 causality errors in the W3 narrative itself; 1 cross-file map label
  inconsistency.
- **B:** **B's W3 file does not finish the world-line.** It runs the
  shared sc-01…sc-08, then the QUIET-AUDIT limb sc-09b…sc-16b, and stops
  at the fork-2 CHOICE card (sc-16b) — the chosen branch (reveal or burn)
  is never rendered. There is no ending. As far as it goes it is
  internally consistent, but it is an incomplete world-line. Count: 0
  causality errors; 1 missing ending (incomplete deliverable).

### 2. Prose quality — told story vs. assembled

- **A: 5.** A's quiet-audit is sustained, voiced narration with strong
  procedural texture: the night-watch from the dark landing, "he moved
  like a man burying a weapon," the daylight he waits for "standing white
  in the dome aperture." It reads as a told story throughout.
- **B: 3.** Same as the other worlds — competent prose interleaved with
  "[QUIET-AUDIT limb …]" labels and "the reader knows whom she shields"
  asides. And it stops mid-structure, so there is materially less B-prose
  to weigh, and what exists is in annotated mode.

### 3. Stakes and tension

- **A: 4.** The patient-watch structure builds a different, colder
  tension (the unwarned guilty man burying his weapon while watched);
  the payoff is methodical rather than explosive, fitting the "quiet"
  brief.
- **B: 3.** B builds tension well up to the hinge (the drawer Pike
  "visits but cannot destroy" is an excellent quiet beat) but then
  *stops at the hinge* — the tension is raised and never discharged.
  An unresolved cliff is not the same as sustained tension; it is
  withheld payoff.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

- **A: 5.** Every setup pays off, and the quiet road's distinctive
  payoff — a case built entirely on mute evidence with "nothing a man
  could take back in the morning" — is thematically satisfying and
  cleanly delivered.
- **B: 2.** B sets up its threads (drawer, log, key, blank plate,
  resignation) but never pays them off — the file ends at the choice card
  before any resolution. Setups without payoff.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

- **A: 5.** The whole point of A's quiet road is epistemic discipline:
  Hale reasons only from observed objects and the man's own figures, and
  the text makes that its thesis. No over-reach.
- **B: 4.** What is present is believable (the drawer-visit, Junia's
  approach), but with no ending there is no final action to test against
  the knowledge ceiling. Scored on the rendered portion only.

### 6. Forced choice for World 3: **A**

This is the clearest of the three. A delivers a complete, well-voiced,
causally airtight quiet-audit world-line; B delivers roughly two-thirds
of one and stops at the fork without rendering the chosen branch or any
ending. Even setting completeness aside, A's prose is in told-story mode
and B's is in annotated-document mode — but completeness cannot be set
aside, because half the judged criteria (payoff satisfaction, ending,
knowledge-at-the-climax) require an ending B does not supply. World 3 to
**A**, decisively.

---

## OVERALL FORCED CHOICE: **A**

Across all three matched world-lines I choose **A**. The decisive axis is
the one the task centers: A reads as a *told story with a consistent
voice*, while B reads as an *assembled, self-annotating artifact*. B
repeatedly surfaces its own machinery — bracketed scene-function labels
("[Dramatic irony …]", "[CONFRONT limb. The spare key.]"), direct
address to "the reader," explicit world-line/ending maps, and closing
"[All six setups paid: …]" answer keys — all of which are the literal
signature of construction rather than narration. A states the same
clues, lands the same reversals, and observes the same epistemic
discipline (no character acts on what they cannot know; Hale reasons only
from what is in his book), but does it *through* prose rather than
*about* it, and in the two emotional hinge scenes (World 2's burn, World
3's night-watch) it produces the best-voiced passages in the entire set.
B's genuine strengths are real but narrow: its setup/payoff bookkeeping
is the tightest, and its "agent unestablished" certification in the burn
line is the single cleanest piece of reasoning. Those wins are
outweighed by two facts — B's tightness is largely a function of writing
its own answer key (artless), and B's World 3 deliverable is incomplete,
ending at a fork with no resolution. A wins all three world-lines and the
overall.
