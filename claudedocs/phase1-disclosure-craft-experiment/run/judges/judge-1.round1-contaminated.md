# Judge 1 — Verdict

Three matched world-line pairs of the same branching mystery ("The Vane
Observatory" / Hale the adjuster). For each world-line I read both A and B,
then scored prose, stakes, setup/payoff, character-knowledge believability,
and made a forced choice. An overall forced choice follows.

Note on the inputs: the version files do not split cleanly one-world-per-file.
B groups its world-lines differently from A (B's `W1` file carries
CONFRONT→fork through sc-16 plus the REVEAL ending; B's `W2` file carries the
BURN world-line ending plus the world-map; B's `W3` file carries the
QUIET-AUDIT limb). A's three files each carry the shared spine sc-01…sc-08 plus
that world-line's distinct limb. I judged each matched pair on the world-line
its filename names, reading the shared spine where both versions present it and
the divergent limb each version supplies for that world-line.

---

## WORLD 1 — confront → reveal (`W1-confront-reveal__A.md` vs `__B.md`)

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

**Version A (W1 file):** I found no hard causality errors inside the
CONFRONT→REVEAL spine this file actually contains. One structural problem: the
file is mislabeled against its content. The `W1-confront-reveal__A.md` file ends
at the *fork-2 choice* (sc-16, the REVEAL/BURN branch point, lines 170-175) and
then pastes in a `[QUIET-AUDIT limb]` block (sc-09b…sc-12b, lines 179-211) and a
trailing `[BURN limb…]` stub (line 213). So the file named "confront-reveal" does
not actually deliver the confront-reveal *ending* — it stops at the choice and
then serves a different world-line's scenes. As a reading artifact for "World 1 =
confront→reveal," A is incomplete: the reveal payoff scene (sc-17r) is absent
from this file. Count: 1 structural/continuity defect (mislabeled-and-truncated
world-line, sc-16 → wrong limb appended).

**Version B (W1 file):** Internally consistent across the spine and the
CONFRONT limb through fork-2, and it *does* carry the REVEAL ending (sc-17,
sc-18, sc-19, sc-20) in this file. I found no causality errors. Minor:
sc-18 attributes the clock stopping to Pike's drowning grip on the works ("a
thread of his sleeve in the works," line 325), which is a *different* mechanism
than A's spine, where the clock is "struck" by the shock of the fall transmitted
through shared iron framing (A sc-14, lines 150-154). These are two different
causal explanations of the same 3:14 clue across the two versions — not an
internal error in either, but the versions disagree on the physics. B's
eyewitness-grip explanation and A's structural-shock explanation are mutually
exclusive accounts of clue (3). Count for B: 0 internal errors.

### 2. Prose quality (told story vs assembled facts)

**A: 5/5.** A is sustained, voiced narration. Long periodic sentences, a
consistent adjuster's interiority ("let the cold do his counting for him"; "neat
thoughts were how adjusters were fooled"), free-indirect access to Junia and
Pike. It reads as one authorial voice telling a story.

**B: 5/5.** B is also genuinely told — arguably more *scenic*, with live dialogue
(the clock exchange, sc-03 lines 41-49; "So am I, of figures") and a deliberate
dramatic-irony narrator who addresses the reader ("Remember that the reader knows
this, and Hale… does not," line 63). Different register from A — more dialogue and
direct reader-address, less dense interiority — but unmistakably a told story, not
a list of facts.

### 3. Stakes / tension

**A: 4/5.** Strong dread and accumulation, but as delivered in *this file* the
world-line's climactic confrontation/reveal is not present, so the tension crests
at the fork and is not discharged. The QUIET-AUDIT material appended afterward
belongs to another line and dissipates the W1 arc.

**B: 5/5.** B builds tension through live confrontation (Pike cornered, the word
"recall" pried out like a tooth, sc-10 line 181-185) and pays it off in the
eyewitness reveal (Junia's gallery account, sc-17-18) and Pike's breaking
("He didn't know?", sc-19 line 338). The arc completes inside the file.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

**A: 3/5.** The shared spine plants all six clues beautifully (night-log hand,
blank plate, stopped clock, drained account, lost key, unsent resignation). But
in the file as delivered for World 1, the confront→reveal payoffs (who climbed
the stair, named) never arrive — the file truncates at the choice. The six
setups are *planted* but not *paid* within this artifact.

**B: 5/5.** B carries an explicit payoff ledger (sc-20 closing bracket, line
357) and actually discharges all six setups on-page: night-log = Junia's hand;
blank plate = she was on the gallery; clock 3:14 = Pike's grip + sleeve-thread;
drained account = motive, confessed; lost-and-found key = Pike's means; unsent
resignation = the central irony. Every plant lands.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

**A: 5/5.** Scrupulous. The whole spine is built on who-knows-what discipline:
Hale catalogues clues he cannot yet read; Junia knows the tread but Hale doesn't;
the omniscient narrator flags exactly what Hale "did not yet" understand. No one
acts on knowledge they lack.

**B: 5/5.** Equally rigorous, and B foregrounds it as the *theme*: the dramatic
irony is explicitly that Pike acts on a false belief (that Crane read the recall)
that the reader knows and Hale doesn't; Junia lies "smoothly true"; the central
tragedy is Pike killing over knowledge Crane never had. The epistemic bookkeeping
is the engine of the story and it never slips.

### 6. Forced choice — World 1: **B**

For the confront→reveal world-line specifically, B is the clear winner because B
actually *delivers* this world-line. The A file named for this line stops at the
fork-2 choice and then pastes in QUIET-AUDIT scenes, so the named payoff (the
reveal, the named climber, the certification) is missing from the artifact. B
not only completes the arc but does so with live confrontation, a true eyewitness
reveal, and a clean six-for-six payoff ledger. On craft the two are close — A's
prose is denser and more interior — but a mystery world-line that does not pay
off its own reveal cannot win the matched comparison for that world-line.

## WORLD 2 — confront → burn (`W2-confront-burn__A.md` vs `__B.md`)

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

**A (W2 file):** This file is, again, the shared spine sc-01…sc-08 followed by
the CONFRONT→REVEAL limb (sc-09a…sc-16) and then a QUIET-AUDIT block — it does
*not* contain the BURN ending the filename promises. The W2 file's tail (lines
185-199) is the QUIET-AUDIT *burn* variant (sc-12b → burn → "death by fall; cause
undetermined" certificate), not a confront→burn ending. So A's "confront-burn"
file actually serves a confront-spine plus a quiet-audit-burn ending. Count: 1
structural defect (filename/content mismatch; the confront→burn ending sc-17w is
not in this file).

**B (W2 file):** B's `W2-confront-burn__B.md` carries the BURN world-line proper —
sc-17b (Junia burns the log, lines 295-306), sc-18b (Hale reasons on mute
evidence, lines 309-319), sc-19b (the "Certified Unknown" ending, lines 323-337),
plus a clean world-line/ending map (lines 341-345). Internally consistent. The
one thing worth flagging: B's map (line 344) defines WL-2 as
"AUDIT QUIETLY → REVEAL," and WL-3 as "(CONFRONT or AUDIT) → BURN" — so B's own
taxonomy folds both burn paths into one ending ("either fork-1 → BURN," line
327). The file's content (a burn ending reachable from confront) matches the
"confront-burn" filename. Count for B: 0 internal errors.

### 2. Prose quality

**A: 5/5.** Same sustained, dense, voiced narration as the shared spine. The
QUIET-AUDIT-burn tail is well written ("the spent calm of a person who has paid a
price on purpose," sc-17w line 189; "the one that could have spoken the last word
was ash before his eyes").

**B: 5/5.** B's burn material is strong, with a standout image-logic: Hale lets
the book burn because "the name lived in memory alone. The book was only its
shadow" (sc-17b line 301). The "Certified Unknown" ending ("the most honest
sentence he had ever signed, and the least use to anybody," line 335) is a real
told-story close with a thesis.

### 3. Stakes / tension

**A: 4/5.** The burn moment in A's tail is quiet and effective — Hale *not*
lunging, the log going up "all at once" — but because A reaches this burn via the
quiet-audit limb rather than the confront limb the filename names, the specific
confront→burn tension (a cornered, confessed Pike, then the witness destroyed) is
not what's delivered. Tension is real but it's the wrong arc's tension.

**B: 5/5.** B's burn lands with the highest-stakes framing of any ending across
all files: an honest adjuster who can prove everything except the one human act,
choosing not to write "accident" and not to name a man he cannot prove — "a hole
exactly the size of a name" (sc-18b line 317). The certification refusal is the
strongest dramatic payoff of the burn premise.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

**A: 3/5.** A's quiet-audit-burn certificate does pay off several clues partially
(fraud cold, key as means without a man, plate as the broken alibi) but
explicitly *cannot* close the death — "death by fall; cause undetermined" — which
is faithful to the burn premise, but as the "confront-burn" artifact it doesn't
discharge the confront-limb setups (the pried-out "recall," the confessed Pike).

**B: 5/5.** B again ships an explicit six-setup payoff ledger for the burn line
(sc-19b bracket, line 337): every clue is certified, the death is ruled
"something else… agent unestablished," and the unsent-resignation irony is
preserved even without a name. The burn premise (witness destroyed) is honored
*and* every plant is accounted for.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

**A: 5/5.** Consistent; Junia burns to protect a name Hale cannot extract, which
is exactly motivated by what she knows and he doesn't.

**B: 5/5.** B makes the epistemic gap the explicit subject of the burn ending —
the reader holds S1 and S2 entire while Hale builds "only as far as the physical
facts will carry him" (sc-18b header, line 311). Nobody acts on what they cannot
know; the whole ending is *about* that boundary.

### 6. Forced choice — World 2: **B**

Same decisive reason as World 1, sharpened: the A file labeled "confront-burn"
does not contain a confront→burn ending — it delivers the confront spine and then
a quiet-audit-burn certificate. B's file actually delivers a burn world-line, with
the strongest single ending in the whole set ("The Certified Unknown" — an
adjuster who proves the fraud, refuses both "accident" and an unprovable name, and
carries the honest non-answer "down into the valley like a stone"). B both
completes the named arc and pays its setups. B wins World 2.

## WORLD 3 — quiet (`W3-quiet__A.md` vs `__B.md`)

### 1. Continuity / causality errors

**A (W3 file):** This is the strongest-built of A's three files and the most
internally coherent. It carries the shared spine sc-01…sc-08, then the
QUIET-AUDIT limb done as a true *watch-and-reconstruct* sequence: Hale declines to
confront (sc-09 reframed, lines 90-92), watches Pike creep out at night (sc, lines
98-101), watches him use the "lost" key on the lower dome door (lines 106-110),
follows him burying the duplicate books (lines 116-118), then independently
re-derives the blank plate (lines 126-132), the struck clock (line 140), the
innocent night-log (line 142), and the undelivered wire + unsent resignation
(lines 150-154), and certifies "a fall, occasioned by… unlawful and unobserved
intrusion" (sc, line 163). It also ships a world-line/ending map (lines 166-170).
I found no causality errors. One discrepancy with the shared spine of the other
files: this A file's spine (sc-09…) is the *confront* fork-1 in the other A files
but here the same sc-08 fork resolves to QUIET-AUDIT — which is correct for a
"quiet" world-line. Count: 0 errors. Internally the cleanest of A's three.

**B (W3 file):** B's `W3-quiet__B.md` carries the shared spine sc-01…sc-08 (which
in B includes the dramatic-irony interludes sc-04 and sc-08, the doubled "fear"
scene) and then the QUIET-AUDIT limb sc-09b…sc-16b, ending at the *fork-2 choice*
(REVEAL/BURN, lines 265-271). So B's quiet file stops at the choice and does NOT
deliver a quiet-world-line *ending* — it routes the reader onward to sc-17 (shared
reveal) or sc-17b (shared burn), neither of which is in this file. As an artifact
for "World 3 = quiet," B is incomplete: no ending scene present. Count: 1
structural defect (truncated at fork-2, no ending in-file).

### 2. Prose quality

**A: 5/5.** Same dense, voiced narration, and here it's used for the best
material of A's three files: the night-watch surveillance ("a man carrying
something he does not want seen has a particular stoop to him," line 100) and the
methodical self-proving close ("there was nothing here a man could take back in
the morning," line 162) give the quiet line a distinct, satisfying restraint.

**B: 5/5.** B's quiet limb is excellent — the "visiting it, not hiding it" beat
is the best psychological observation in either version's quiet line ("He was not
hiding evidence… He was visiting it. Whatever was in that drawer, Pike both could
not part with it and could not bear it," sc, lines 175-179). The cruelty-dressed-
as-courtesy drawer inventory (line 213) is sharp. Prose-for-prose, B's quiet limb
edges A's slightly in psychological texture — but it stops before an ending.

### 3. Stakes / tension

**A: 5/5.** A's quiet line sustains real tension (the silent night-pursuit down
the iron stair, the held watch in the dark) AND discharges it in a complete,
distinctive ending — a case proved entirely on mute objects, "having spoken to no
one and proved everything" (line 164). The quiet road pays off as a *complete*
dramatic statement.

**B: 4/5.** B's quiet limb generates strong tension (the drawer vigil, the
stratagem) but, as delivered, releases none of it: the file ends on Junia's
fingers curling on the book at the fork (line 261). The reader is left at the
hinge with no resolution in this artifact.

### 4. Setup / payoff satisfaction

**A: 5/5.** A's quiet file pays off all six setups on-page and ties them to the
watch-and-reconstruct method: blank plate (alibi broken, lines 126-132), struck
clock (line 140), innocent night-log re-reasoned without Junia's help (line 142),
drained account (proven guinea by guinea, line 92), kept key (witnessed warm in
Pike's coat, lines 106-110), undelivered wire + unsent resignation (lines
150-154). The certificate (line 163) discharges the death. Complete.

**B: 2/5.** B's quiet file plants everything via the careful audit (drawer, key
behind clock with sleeve-thread, night-log in Junia's hand, resignation by
stratagem) — genuinely well-seeded — but pays off *nothing*, because the file ends
at the fork-2 choice. No reveal, no burn, no certificate within this artifact.

### 5. Character-knowledge believability

**A: 5/5.** Exemplary, and the quiet method *foregrounds* epistemic discipline:
Hale deliberately builds a case "carried back to a thing he had seen or read and
never once to a word any frightened man had confessed" (line 154). Knowledge is
scrupulously sourced.

**B: 5/5.** Equally careful — the quiet limb's whole virtue is that Hale "leaned
on no one's word"; the reader's fuller frame (S1/S2 unspent) is held against
Hale's patiently-built but name-less theory (sc, line 251). No knowledge
violations.

### 6. Forced choice — World 3: **A**

This is the one world-line where A wins, and decisively, for the symmetric reason
B won the other two: completeness. A's `W3-quiet` file delivers a *whole* quiet
world-line — the distinctive surveillance arc, the all-objects-no-confession
proof, and a complete certification ending — and pays all six setups. B's
`W3-quiet` file is the truncated half: a beautifully observed quiet limb (its
"visiting, not hiding" beat is the finest psychological moment in the set) that
stops at the fork-2 choice with no ending and no payoffs in the artifact. On the
*shared* craft (prose, character-knowledge) the two are even, and B's quiet
psychology is a touch richer line-by-line — but for World 3 as a deliverable
story, A is the only complete one.

---

## OVERALL FORCED CHOICE: **B**

Across the whole branching story I choose **B**.

The cleanest way to see it: each version delivers complete *endings* for two of
the three world-lines and truncates the third at a fork. A completes the QUIET
line (and, in its other two files, re-serves spine + a partial/mismatched limb,
leaving the confront→reveal and confront→burn *endings* absent from their named
files). B completes the REVEAL and BURN lines (with explicit, fully-discharged
six-setup payoff ledgers and the strongest single endings in the set — the "Loud
Certification" and especially the "Certified Unknown"), and truncates only the
QUIET line at its fork. So B ships two finished, high-payoff world-line endings to
A's one.

On pure prose-craft the two are near-equal — both read as genuinely told stories
with a consistent voice and rigorous who-knows-what discipline; A leans denser and
more interior, B leans more scenic with live dialogue and an explicit dramatic-
irony narrator. That stylistic difference is a wash. What breaks the tie is
delivered payoff: B's matched files actually pay off their setups and land their
endings on two of three world-lines, including the two endings (Pike's broken
"He didn't know?" and Hale's "something else, agent unestablished") that give the
whole branching premise its tragic point. B also makes the epistemic-frame
collision — the engine of the mystery — its overt subject and resolves it on the
page. For the story as a whole, B is the more complete and more satisfying
delivery; A's standout is the single QUIET world-line, which is not enough to
carry the overall.
